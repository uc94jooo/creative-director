# Mom Song (Old Spice · 2012)

## Insight
Mothers buy grooming products for their sons, then sons grow up and a girlfriend or wife buys them differently. The transition from "boy who smells like my son" to "man who smells like someone else's man" is a genuine and underexplored maternal grief.

## Mechanic
A Super Bowl film in which mothers sing a lament as their sons leave home and begin dating — the grooming product transition dramatised as a comedic operatic tragedy. Absurd production values (theatrical staging, weeping mothers, operatic delivery) are played completely straight. Part of the "Smellcome to Manhood" platform.

## Why it worked
Absurd as carrier (P11): the operatic staging applied to the mundane reality of a grooming product purchase creates a comedic gap that is both funny and genuinely tender. Long-form drama (P16): the extended musical format is only possible with premium production and timing; it earns its length through the joke's escalation. The emotion (maternal separation anxiety) is real even when the execution is absurd.

## Steal
Apply a maximalist artistic form (opera, ballet, Greek tragedy) to a minimal consumer truth and play it completely straight. The contrast between form and subject is the comedy. The underlying emotion must be genuine or the joke collapses.
