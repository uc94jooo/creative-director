# Revenge (Clash of Clans · 2015)

## Insight
Mobile gaming has a stigma — "it's just a game." But anyone who's been raided in Clash of Clans knows the sting of real competitive loss. The emotional investment is genuine; the audience just won't admit it openly.

## Mechanic
A Super Bowl ad featuring Liam Neeson as a furious player recording a voice memo to a stranger who destroyed his village in-game — channeling his "Taken" persona into a mobile gaming revenge fantasy. Played completely straight for maximum comedic impact.

## Why it worked
The joke works because the absurdity is grounded in real emotion. Liam Neeson's cinematic intensity applied to a mobile game validated the feeling every player has after a raid without explaining it. The Taken parody made it shareable beyond the gaming audience.

## Steal
When the product experience contains a real emotion the audience is embarrassed to admit, cast it with cultural credibility and play it completely straight. The comedy comes from the gap between the gravitas and the context, not from winking at the audience.
