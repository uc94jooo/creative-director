# Re2pect (Jordan Brand · 2014)

## Insight
Derek Jeter's retirement was not a sporting event — it was a cultural goodbye to a figure who transcended rivalry. Across 20 years of competition, every player, fan, and city had a specific relationship with Jeter that required acknowledgment. The campaign's job was to hold space for that complexity.

## Mechanic
A two-minute film set during Jeter's final season — showing opponent players, coaches, fans, and stadium workers giving him standing ovations and tips of the cap city by city. No narration. No product. The brand is present only as the cultural custodian of the moment. Title: "Re2pect" — using Jeter's uniform number 2 as punctuation.

## Why it worked
P16 (long-form drama) distilled to its purest form: let the subject carry the emotion, remove everything else. The brand's restraint — Jordan does not appear in the film beyond a brief logo — amplified the sincerity. The typography choice (Re2pect) was the only creative act needed; everything else was documentary reality. A genuinely unique cultural moment, irreproducible.

## Steal
When a public moment is already emotionally loaded, a brand's job is curation, not creation. The creative brief is: what is the minimum editorial frame that lets the moment breathe? Remove everything beyond that minimum.
