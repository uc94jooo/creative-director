# Cars to Work (Renault · 2024)

## Insight
The societal and political pressure to drive less treats car ownership as a lifestyle choice. For 54% of people in vulnerable situations in France, a car is not a choice — it is the only way to access employment in mobility deserts. They are losing job offers not because they can't do the work but because they can't get there. A car maker facing pressure to justify car use has the most powerful argument available: this car is how someone gets their life back.

## Mechanic
Renault created a solidarity initiative that lends cars to unemployed people during their trial periods at new jobs — with payment starting only once their position is secured. The car comes before the income; the income becomes possible because of the car. The program targets people in mobility deserts: areas of France where public transport cannot reach employment centers. The campaign is a national project operating through social welfare partnerships, not a promotional incentive — it is a structural response to a structural problem.

## Why it worked
P07 (Utility as Brand): the brand's product solves the specific human problem at the center of the campaign — this is not a metaphor, the car literally enables employment. P17 (Cause as Core): in the current cultural context where car brands face pressure to defend the existence of cars, Renault found the clearest possible moral argument: there are people whose economic survival depends on access to a car, and a car brand is uniquely positioned to provide that access. The message "this time it's the car that helps you get the job" reframes car use from environmental liability to social necessity without being defensive.

## Steal
When your product category is under cultural or political pressure, find the segment of the population for whom your product is not optional — it is lifesaving or livelihood-enabling. Make that use case the center of your brand purpose. The defensive argument (we're not as bad as they say) is always weaker than the positive argument (this is who we exist for).
