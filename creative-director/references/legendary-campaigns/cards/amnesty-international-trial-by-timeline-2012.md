# Trial by Timeline (Amnesty International · 2012)

## Insight
In repressive regimes, social media posts are used as evidence of political opposition — making a Facebook timeline the equivalent of a criminal file. Turning that threat into a visible demonstration on the very platform that creates the risk was pointed and immediate.

## Mechanic
A platform that converted users' Facebook timelines into a mock arrest warrant, using their own public posts as "evidence" — the same way authoritarian governments actually do. Users could share their "warrant" to spread awareness of how social media is used as surveillance evidence in repressive states.

## Why it worked
The mechanic was the message. By putting the user in the position of the accused — using their own words as the evidence — Amnesty made an abstract international rights issue personal and immediate. The participation was not performative; it was genuinely unsettling.

## Steal
When the injustice you're campaigning against uses a familiar mechanism (social media, data, technology) against people, the most powerful demonstration is turning that same mechanism on your audience — briefly, safely, with permission — so they understand what it feels like.
