# Windows (British Airways · 2024)

## Insight
The most emotionally charged moment of any flight is not the meal, the seat, or the destination — it's the pause at the window: a private, unspoken experience that no airline had ever thought to sell.

## Mechanic
Eleven large-format OOH prints showing close-cropped faces of passengers at aircraft windows, expressions of wonder or reflection. British Airways logo partially cut off by the frame — a deliberate act of brand confidence. 324 placements across the UK.

## Why it worked
Limitation as power (P06): the crop communicates that BA is so recognizable it doesn't need to show its logo in full. Design as idea (P17): the image does all the work — no headline required. The invisible-brand confidence (P07) operates through pure restraint.

## Steal
If your brand is genuinely famous, crop your logo deliberately. The act of truncation communicates status more efficiently than any superlative headline. Weaker competitors cannot imitate it without looking incompetent.
