# I Love Doing Dishes (Finish · 2014)

## Insight
Nobody loves doing dishes. A dishwasher detergent brand that acknowledges this directly — and then provides the relief — is more honest than one that shows people smiling while loading the machine.

## Mechanic
An absurdist film that plays with the complete implausibility of the claim "I love doing dishes" — using that irony to reframe Finish as the product that makes the thing you hate less hateful, rather than pretending you'll enjoy it.

## Why it worked
P11 (absurd as carrier) deployed against the category convention of forced positive product-use scenarios. The self-aware irony is a category inversion in a segment where all competitors show idealized domestic happiness. The brand that admits the task is unpleasant earns more trust than the brand that pretends otherwise.

## Steal
In a category where all brands show aspirational product use, honesty about the mundane reality is a differentiator. The admission "this is a chore" followed by "and we make it less bad" is a stronger sell than "you'll love it."
