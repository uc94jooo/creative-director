# Cat Herders (EDS · 2000)

## Insight
EDS was a B2B IT services company trying to explain complex systems integration to business decision-makers during the first dot-com boom. Its competitors communicated in jargon and abstraction. The insight: what EDS does — managing complex, unruly, independent-minded systems across an enterprise — is exactly like herding cats.

## Mechanic
A Super Bowl spot shot as a western documentary — cattlemen on horseback, sweeping cinematography, dramatic music — herding thousands of cats across an American prairie. Cowboys nurse scratched hands, brush cat hair off their jackets, and stare into the sunset with quiet pride. Tagline: "EDS. Managing the complex. Moving business forward." Played entirely straight, as if cat herding were a noble profession.

## Why it worked
The absurdism was load-bearing strategy, not entertainment: the visual metaphor of cat herding communicated EDS's actual value proposition more clearly than any product description could. Decision-makers who recognized the chaos of enterprise IT immediately understood the analogy. The western film format gave the work dignity and scale — EDS appeared competent and serious even through comedy. Running this in the Super Bowl, then the most expensive advertising slot in the world, signaled to B2B buyers that EDS was a legitimate, substantial company. The meta-message of the spend was as important as the message of the spot.

## Steal
When a B2B value proposition is complex and abstract, find the physical metaphor that makes it immediately visceral. The best B2B metaphors have two qualities: they describe the problem (herding cats = chaos) and the solution (expert herders = EDS manages the chaos) simultaneously.
