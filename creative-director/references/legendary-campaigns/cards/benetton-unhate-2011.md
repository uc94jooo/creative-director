# Unhate (Benetton · 2011)

## Insight
Political enemies share more humanity than their public conflict suggests. The photographic image of enemy leaders kissing — an act of reconciliation — is impossible in reality but imaginable in design. If hatred is a learned behavior, "unhate" is the counter-program.

## Mechanic
A series of photomontage images showing world leaders who are publicly opposed kissing each other on the lips — Barack Obama and Hu Jintao, Nicolas Sarkozy and Angela Merkel, Benjamin Netanyahu and Mahmoud Abbas, Pope Benedict XVI and Ahmed Mohamed el-Tayeb. The images were released as global billboard campaign and online simultaneously. Pope image pulled after Vatican protest. The controversy generated tens of millions of media impressions.

## Why it worked
P17 (design as idea): the photomontage is the idea — no copy needed beyond the campaign word "Unhate." The design decision (compositing world leaders kissing) is itself the message. P13 (truth telling): the images say something true and uncomfortable about the performative nature of political enmity while presenting an impossible image. Benetton had the authentic brand standing to do this — decades of provocative social campaigns made this credible rather than opportunistic.

## Steal
Find the image that is impossible in reality but logically necessary as a statement. The world leaders kissing cannot happen in reality — which is precisely why it is effective as a designed image. The design gap between reality and the image is where the message lives.
