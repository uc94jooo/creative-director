# Hot Dog (Miller Genuine Draft · 2000)

## Insight
American food identity is deeply personal and often irrational. The hot dog — emblematic of backyard culture, ballparks, summertime — carries more emotional weight than its simplicity suggests. Beer and hot dogs share a ritual context that neither exploits directly.

## Mechanic
An Errol Morris documentary portrait featuring a character with strongly held convictions about hot dogs — their preparation, their meaning, their specific requirements — delivered with full earnestness. Part of the Miller Genuine Draft Errol Morris series.

## Why it worked
The specificity of food opinion reveals character more than biography. A person with strong hot dog opinions is knowable and likable in a way that a person with generic opinions is not. The beer associates with authenticity by surrounding itself with genuinely specific people.

## Steal
Find the most specific, mundane opinion your target audience holds and make it the center of a portrait. The specificity is the charm. Any level of aspiration or abstraction kills it.
