# Our Blades Are F***ing Great (Dollar Shave Club · 2012)

## Insight
The shaving category was dominated by Gillette's over-engineered, over-priced hardware. Consumers knew they were being gouged but had no credible alternative. A DTC startup with no media budget needed to detonate the category with a single shot.

## Mechanic
A $4,500 walk-and-talk film: CEO Michael Dubin strides through the warehouse making absurdist jokes, mocking overpriced blades, and promising blades that are "fucking great" for $1/month. No production value. No agency. Pure founder voice. 12,000 orders in the first 48 hours. 27M YouTube views in 60 days.

## Why it worked
Absurd as carrier (P11): comedy disarmed skepticism in a category built on pseudo-science claims. Long-form drama (P16): the two-minute run time was justified by laugh density — every sentence earned its stay. The founder's face as brand asset is irreplaceable; it made every claim feel like a personal warranty. The profanity was functional: it signaled "we will tell you the truth."

## Steal
Founder-led video with genuine humor is the cheapest and most defensible DTC launch vehicle. Low production value signals authenticity only if the writing is sharp enough to carry it. Profanity works as a credibility marker, not a shock tactic, when the product claim behind it is real.
