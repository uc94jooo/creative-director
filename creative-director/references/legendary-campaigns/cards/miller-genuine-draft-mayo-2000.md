# Mayo (Miller Genuine Draft · 2000)

## Insight
American beer culture is built on occasion and ritual — not taste. The most interesting material for beer advertising is not the beer itself but the people who drink it and what they believe.

## Mechanic
A short Errol Morris character portrait featuring an individual with an unusual or strongly held opinion — possibly about mayonnaise or food — delivered with the quiet sincerity of a documentary interview. Part of the Miller Genuine Draft Errol Morris series.

## Why it worked
The specificity of opinion (even a banal one) creates a distinctly human portrait that is more memorable than a product demonstration. Morris's framing technique — interviewees speaking directly to camera — creates intimacy that advertising rarely achieves.

## Steal
Commission a documentarian, not an advertising director, for a character-led campaign. The grammar of documentary creates a different contract with the viewer: they believe what they see in ways they cannot believe a scripted commercial.
