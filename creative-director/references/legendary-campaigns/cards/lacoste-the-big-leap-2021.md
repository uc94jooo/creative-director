# The Big Leap (Lacoste · 2021)

## Insight
Life's pivotal moments feel like stepping off a cliff — the moment between commitment and consequence, when everything could go either way.

## Mechanic
A film built around the surreal, extended visual of characters leaping — the absurd logic (P11) of an impossible, elongated fall becomes a metaphor for life's risks. Long form allows the absurdist premise to breathe and accumulate meaning rather than land as a punchline.

## Why it worked
Lacoste's consistent long-form investment signals brand confidence. The absurdist visual language (P11) differentiates from the earnest competitor films in fashion, while the emotional undertow of risk-and-reward grounds the surrealism in something universally felt.

## Steal
Absurdism works in luxury when it's internally consistent — one surreal premise followed to its logical visual conclusion without explanation. The moment a fashion brand explains the metaphor, the spell breaks.
