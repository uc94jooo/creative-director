# Meet Marina Prieto (JCDecaux · 2024)

## Insight
Loneliness among the elderly is invisible — it has no image, no protest, no campaign. Marina Prieto, 85, placed a handwritten ad in a local shop window looking for friends. It was the most honest advertising anyone had seen.

## Mechanic
JCDecaux, an outdoor advertising company, amplified Marina's handwritten ad onto their billboards across Spain. The same handwritten font, the same personal tone, the same phone number — but now visible to millions. The response was overwhelming: calls, letters, visits, new friendships.

## Why it worked
The truth-telling (P13) is Marina's own voice, unchanged and amplified. JCDecaux's role is pure infrastructure — they provided the scale without altering the message. The participation mechanic (P09): the audience's response (calling Marina) IS the campaign's success metric. The brand demonstrates its power by disappearing behind someone else's story.

## Steal
Find the most honest ad that already exists in the world — handwritten, undesigned, urgent. Amplify it without changing a word. Your production budget is invisible; their authenticity is irreplaceable.
