# Playin' With My Friends (IKEA · 2013)

## Insight
IKEA products furnish the spaces where children play and adults socialise — but no one thinks of furniture as the enabler of connection. The insight: the best childhood memories happen in the most ordinary domestic spaces, made extraordinary by who's in them.

## Mechanic
A film showing children playing with IKEA furniture in ways that transform it into imaginative worlds — forts, stages, ships — while a version of "Playin' With My Friends" plays on the soundtrack. The furniture is not the hero; the play it enables is.

## Why it worked
P11 (absurd as carrier) gently — the children's imaginative transformations of ordinary furniture into extraordinary play spaces is the comedy and the truth simultaneously. P16 gives the film enough time to build genuine warmth. IKEA's functional products disappear into the background, which is exactly the right brand move: the furniture is so good at being invisible that the life around it becomes vivid.

## Steal
When your product enables a human behaviour, let the behaviour be the hero and the product be the stage. The most invisible brand communication is often the most effective.
