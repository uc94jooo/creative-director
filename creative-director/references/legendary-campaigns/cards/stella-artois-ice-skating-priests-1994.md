# Ice Skating Priests (Stella Artois · 1994)

## Insight

"Reassuringly Expensive" positions Stella as the beer worth any price or sacrifice. The humor of priests — symbols of self-denial — abandoning propriety for a pint of Stella becomes a compressed argument: even those sworn to abstinence cannot resist.

## Mechanic

A TV film featuring Catholic priests discovering the pleasure of ice skating while presumably fetching Stella Artois. The comedy lies in watching authority figures behave with undignified joy. Part of a long-running UK campaign series that built Stella's premium positioning through absurdist vignettes.

## Why it worked

P11 (absurd as carrier): the priests-on-ice premise bypasses the viewer's resistance to alcohol advertising through pure comic incongruity. P16 (long-form drama) builds the joke slowly enough that the payoff earns its space. The "Reassuringly Expensive" platform proved that premium positioning could be delivered entirely through tone rather than product claims.

## Steal

When the product claim is a price premium, demonstrate that premium through the quality and irreverence of the storytelling — not through aspirational lifestyle imagery. Make the joke expensive.
