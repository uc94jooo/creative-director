# Cook This Page (IKEA · 2017)

## Insight
IKEA sells kitchen products. Recipe books and food print are entirely separate categories. But what if the recipe could be cooked on the page itself — turning an ad into a functional cooking tool?

## Mechanic
IKEA created parchment-paper recipe pages printed with food items at scale. Readers could place the page directly in a baking dish, add the ingredients shown, and cook it. The print ad becomes the recipe card and the baking liner simultaneously.

## Why it worked
Design as idea (P17): the ad's material and format IS the product utility demonstration. Behaviour inversion (P03): the ad is consumed by cooking rather than read. The execution is elegant, cheap to produce, and generates enormous share value through its unexpected functionality.

## Steal
Ask what your ad could DO beyond communicate. When the physical form of the communication can be repurposed as a tool that demonstrates the product's use case, you've found a design idea, not a communication idea.
