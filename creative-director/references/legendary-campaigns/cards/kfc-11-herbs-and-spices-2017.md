# 11 Herbs and Spices (KFC · 2017)

## Insight
The internet's attention economy rewards brands that behave unexpectedly, not brands that broadcast. KFC's secret recipe — 11 herbs and spices — is its most famous mystery. Making that mystery visible through an impossibly tiny gesture was more powerful than any announcement.

## Mechanic
KFC's Twitter account followed exactly 11 people: the five Spice Girls and six men named Herb. A fan spotted this, tweeted about it, and the story went global. KFC did not announce or explain the stunt — they simply waited for discovery. The account had been configured this way for months before anyone noticed.

## Why it worked
P09 (user as co-author): the audience discovered the joke and became the news. P11 (absurd as carrier): the logic is internally perfect — 11 herbs, 11 follows, Herb + Spice Girls — and can be explained in one sentence, but only after you stare at it. Zero paid media. The entire campaign cost the creative idea and the patience to wait. The D&AD Yellow Pencil validated that the smallest possible act, executed with precision, beats the biggest possible media buy.

## Steal
Design for discovery, not for announcement. Hide a perfectly formed joke in plain sight and let the audience be the journalist. The moment of discovery — "wait, did they really do this?" — generates more earned media than any press release.
