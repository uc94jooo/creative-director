# Boost Your Voice (Boost Mobile · 2020)

## Insight
Black communities are systematically underrepresented in elections partly due to polling station closures. Boost Mobile's stores were already located in underserved Black communities. The stores could do something about it.

## Mechanic
Boost Mobile converted its retail stores in underserved communities into polling stations for the 2020 US election. The brand literally used its physical footprint to expand voting access — becoming infrastructure for civic participation.

## Why it worked
Brand as activist (P04): the action was structural, not symbolic — stores became polling stations, not billboards about voting. User as co-author (P09): the community participated by voting; the brand provided the mechanism. The connection between the brand's actual asset (stores in underserved areas) and the social problem (voting access) was airtight.

## Steal
Identify what physical or operational asset your brand owns that could solve a real civic or community problem. The most credible activism converts existing brand infrastructure into social good — not a check or a campaign, but actual function.
