# Wasted (E*Trade · 2008)

## Insight
Paying a financial advisor feels prudent. Realising you could have managed your own investments for a fraction of the cost — with the same or better results — feels like having been conned. The money spent on advisors was "wasted" in the most embarrassing sense.

## Mechanic
Super Bowl spot: a baby, sitting in a home office, talks directly to camera about online investing — and the idiocy of paying someone else to do it. "I just used two-thirds of my diaper budget." The baby is more competent at this than most adults. E*Trade's self-service investing platform endorsed by the youngest possible expert.

## Why it worked
The talking baby externalised the insight (this is so simple even a baby could do it) without stating it directly. The absurdity was the argument. The campaign ran for years, with the baby growing up and becoming a cultural figure.

## Steal
When the product benefit is radical simplicity, find the most extreme version of a naive user who can still operate it effortlessly. The naivety makes the simplicity claim; the execution makes it funny.
