# Whopper Neutrality (Burger King · 2018)

## Insight
Net neutrality is an abstract policy concept that most Americans cannot explain despite it directly affecting their daily internet experience. Burger King could translate it into something everyone understands: paying more to get your Whopper faster.

## Mechanic
Burger King filmed real customers being told they would have to pay more for faster Whopper delivery, with a "fast lane" for premium payers and a "slow lane" for standard. The customers' confusion and frustration mirrored exactly what net neutrality repeal would mean for internet access.

## Why it worked
Cultural hijack (P05): attaches to the net neutrality news cycle. Brand as activist (P04): Burger King takes a political position through product demonstration. The social experiment with real customers (genuine reactions) creates credible evidence. The analogy is perfect: the Whopper as internet data, the restaurant as ISP.

## Steal
When a complex policy issue is in the public conversation, ask whether your brand's product or service is a perfect analogy for explaining it. If yes, run the experiment — with real customers, not actors.
