# D Rose Jump Store (Adidas · 2012)

## Insight
Derrick Rose was one of the most explosive NBA players alive — his signature shoe was about vertical leap and athleticism. To promote the D Rose shoe, Adidas asked: what if you actually had to jump for it? What if the price was not money but performance?

## Mechanic
A pop-up store placed D Rose signature shoes on a shelf at a height matching Rose's jump — 10 feet. Anyone who could jump and retrieve the shoes got them free. The store was filmed, participants tried and sometimes succeeded. The footage circulated organically. No paid media required for the video.

## Why it worked
Product-as-proof (implicit) through a participatory mechanic (P09) packaged as a cultural hijack (P05) — a pop-up store that generated its own media through the challenge. The stunt was perfectly calibrated: the height was achievable for genuinely athletic people, which meant the content was both aspirational and occasionally triumphant. The Derrick Rose connection gave the mechanic celebrity credibility without requiring Rose to appear.

## Steal
When your product is about performance, design the retail or activation mechanic as a performance test. The product becomes the reward for proving you deserve it. The mechanic becomes the campaign.
