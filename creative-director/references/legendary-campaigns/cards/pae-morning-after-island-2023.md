# Morning After Island (PAE · 2023)

## Insight
In Uruguay, access to emergency contraception was legally restricted in some municipalities. A territorial gap in the law created a situation where geography determined reproductive rights.

## Mechanic
PAE (a reproductive health NGO) created an artificial island in the River Uruguay — positioned in international waters between jurisdictions — where access to emergency contraception was legal. The island was built, publicised, and accessible. Its existence made the law's absurdity concrete.

## Why it worked
The stunt is pure activism executed with design precision. Creating the island is more powerful than any protest, campaign, or petition — it demonstrates the problem's solution while exposing the law's arbitrariness. The story requires no explanation: an island exists because a law doesn't.

## Steal
When a law or rule is arbitrary, find the geographic, legal, or technical edge case that makes its arbitrariness visible by actually occupying it. The existence of your solution is the argument against the problem.
