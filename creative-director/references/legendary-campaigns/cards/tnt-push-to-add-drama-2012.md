# Push to Add Drama (TNT · 2012)

## Insight

TNT stands for drama — but in Belgium, no one knew what TNT was. To launch the channel, the brand needed to demonstrate what drama feels like rather than explain what drama is.

## Mechanic

A "Push to Add Drama" button placed in a quiet Belgian square. One person pushes it. Orchestrated chaos erupts: ambulances, motorcycles, fighting, a woman in lingerie on a stretcher, a gang showdown — real actors, real stunts, in the actual street. The reaction of real passersby was filmed and broadcast. The button pushed the drama; the audience couldn't look away.

## Why it worked

Tech as canvas meets user as co-author: the trigger (the button) made an ordinary person the catalyst for extraordinary events — exactly what watching TNT feels like as a viewer. The stunt was the brand demonstration. The shocked faces of real people were the ratings argument. Globally viral without any paid distribution.

## Steal

If your product delivers an emotional experience, design a physical situation that delivers that exact emotion to a real person, in public, unexpectedly. Film their authentic reaction. The reaction is the ad.
