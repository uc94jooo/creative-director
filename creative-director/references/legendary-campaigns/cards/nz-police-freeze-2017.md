# Freeze (New Zealand Police · 2017)

## Insight
Police recruitment advertising is invariably earnest, authoritative, and forgettable. The people least likely to apply are exactly those who distrust institutional seriousness. What if a police recruitment campaign behaved like the opposite of a police recruitment campaign?

## Mechanic
New Zealand Police released a recruitment video where officers perform an elaborate Bollywood-style song and dance sequence, freezing mid-action at intervals to deliver recruitment messages. The video went globally viral and dramatically increased applications.

## Why it worked
The absurdist tone (P11) signals that the NZ Police are not the sterile institution recruits fear. The self-awareness of the joke communicates confidence. People who share the video because it's funny are simultaneously spreading the recruitment message to exactly the demographic the force wants.

## Steal
In institutional categories where earnestness is the default, absurdism signals confidence and honesty. The willingness to look silly means the institution isn't precious about itself — which is exactly the cultural signal that recruits value.
