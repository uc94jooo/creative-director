# Dundee Returns (Tourism Australia · 2018)

## Insight
American tourists would visit Australia if they thought about it, but they don't think about it enough. The greatest Australian cultural export to America was Paul Hogan's Crocodile Dundee. What if there was a sequel?

## Mechanic
A mock Hollywood movie trailer for "Dundee: The Son of a Legend Returns Home" — starring Chris Hemsworth and Danny McBride — that ran in full before the Super Bowl, fooling many viewers into believing a new Dundee film was coming. The reveal: it was a Tourism Australia campaign. The trailer mechanic was the ideal vehicle for reaching American audiences in the Super Bowl context.

## Why it worked
Cultural hijack (P05): hijacking the Hollywood blockbuster trailer format — with genuine stars — earned attention and credibility normally unavailable to a tourist board. Absurd as carrier (P11): the joke lands precisely because Chris Hemsworth cannot play Paul Hogan's son, but plays it straight. The reveal extended the campaign's life through earned media coverage of "the hoax."

## Steal
Adopt a format that carries more cultural authority than your category normally commands — the film trailer, the tech keynote, the award acceptance speech. Authenticity of execution is everything; if the fake trailer looks like a real trailer, the joke works. If it looks like an ad, it fails.
