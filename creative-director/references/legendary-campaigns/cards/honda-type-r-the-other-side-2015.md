# The Other Side (Honda · 2015)

## Insight
Most people drive two versions of themselves: the responsible daily driver and the driver who secretly wants more. The Civic Type R exists for the second driver — but Honda had to prove it without alienating the first.

## Mechanic
A dual-narrative film where pressing the letter "R" on the keyboard instantly switches between two parallel lives: a mild-mannered family man in a standard Civic, and the same driver as a criminal getaway driver in a Type R. The films run simultaneously; the viewer controls which story they're in.

## Why it worked
P03 (behavior inversion): Honda — the most sensible car brand — built an ad that showed its product in a getaway from the police, told as a crime thriller. P16 gave the narrative enough cinematic quality to make both stories genuinely gripping. The interactive mechanic (pressing "R") was not a gimmick but the idea itself: the choice between the two lives is exactly the emotional territory the Type R occupies. This is one of the few interactive automotive ads that earned the format.

## Steal
When a product has a dual personality — practical and thrilling — don't pick one. Build a mechanic that lets the audience inhabit both simultaneously. The choice itself is the insight.
