# With 10 Years of Life (Toshiba · 2012)

## Insight
Electronic devices are disposable by design — planned obsolescence is the industry norm. Toshiba's positioning around durability and longevity was a genuine differentiation against competitors. But "10 years" as a spec is abstract; the emotional meaning of 10 years — what changes in a life over that period — is concrete and human.

## Mechanic
A long-form film showing what 10 years of a life looks like: a character (or family) tracked through a decade of changes — relationships forming and breaking, children growing, career shifts, loss and joy. Throughout, a Toshiba device is present. The P13 truth: 10 years is not a spec; it is a significant portion of a human life, and the things that are with us for 10 years become part of our story.

## Why it worked
P16 (long-form drama): 10 years as a narrative requires the scope that only long-form allows. P13 (truth telling): the truth being told is that we measure product durability in spec numbers but experience it in life events. A 10-year device witnessed a decade of a human life — that is not a technical claim; it is a human claim.

## Steal
Convert technical specifications into their human equivalents. "10 years" = a child growing from infant to primary school; a marriage forming and maturing; a career changing course. The specification tells you nothing about why you should care; the human equivalent tells you everything.
