# Interface Interruption (Tubi · 2023)

## Insight
The Super Bowl ad break is the most jealously guarded viewing ritual in American culture — nobody touches the remote. Tubi, a free streaming challenger, couldn't buy the same ad volume as established competitors. So they made everyone else's ad time work for them.

## Mechanic
During the Super Bowl broadcast, Tubi ran a 15-second spot that precisely mimicked the UI of a Tubi app accidentally opening on a TV — showing the streaming interface appearing to "take over" the broadcast. Millions of viewers thought someone had grabbed the remote. Social media exploded.

## Why it worked
Format as idea (P01): the UI simulation was only possible because Tubi understood exactly how its own interface would read as a disruption on a living-room television. Absurdism (P11): the premise was technically absurd but executed with enough visual fidelity to create genuine confusion. The mechanic cost a fraction of a typical Super Bowl spot and generated coverage equivalent to a much larger media buy.

## Steal
Instead of competing for attention in a crowded format, attack the ritual surrounding the format. The Super Bowl remote-touching taboo was the free asset — Tubi just needed to trigger it.
