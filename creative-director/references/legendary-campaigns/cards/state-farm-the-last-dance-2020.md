# The Last Dance (State Farm · 2020)

## Insight
The Michael Jordan documentary aired during lockdown — a moment of collective identity crisis for Americans. State Farm, which had appeared in ESPN broadcasts throughout the Jordan era, had an authentic archival presence in that cultural memory.

## Mechanic
A deepfake-enhanced recreation of an ESPN segment from 1998 in which "commentators of the time" discussed the future — which was the audience's present. State Farm was already there then and is still there now: the through-line is brand permanence. The segment was designed to blend seamlessly with the documentary's archival footage.

## Why it worked
Insurance is a category where stability is the core value proposition but the most boring thing to say. By embedding the brand inside a moment of authenticated nostalgia — using the documentary's own grammar — State Farm proved permanence without asserting it. The deepfake technique was invisible enough to create confusion and visible enough to create conversation.

## Steal
Find a moment in the cultural or category archive where your brand was actually present. Re-stage it so that nostalgia becomes a structural argument for reliability rather than a sentimental ad. The archive is proof; staged sincerity is not.
