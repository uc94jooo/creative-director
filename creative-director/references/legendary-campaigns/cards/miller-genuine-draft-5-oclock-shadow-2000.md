# 5 O'Clock Shadow (Miller Genuine Draft · 2000)

## Insight
The moment the workday ends and working-class identity reasserts itself is a ritual unmarked by beer advertising, which typically shows the party, not the transition. The 5 o'clock shadow — the physical marker of a day's labor — is invisible in the category.

## Mechanic
An Errol Morris documentary portrait exploring masculinity, work, and the end-of-day ritual through a character whose identity is partly expressed through their 5 o'clock shadow. Part of the Miller Genuine Draft character series.

## Why it worked
The physical detail (five o'clock shadow) anchors an emotional truth about working identity and the reward of evening. Morris's observational style made the ordinary feel significant without sentimentalizing it.

## Steal
Beer's most honest advertising moment is not the celebration but the transition — the moment before the first sip, when the day ends and something shifts. That liminal moment is unowned territory in most markets.
