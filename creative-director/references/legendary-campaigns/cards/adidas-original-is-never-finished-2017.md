# Original is Never Finished (Adidas Originals · 2017)

## Insight
The word "original" in cultural discourse doesn't mean "the first" — it means "the source from which everything else is derived." Adidas Originals is not the first sneaker; it is the one that every subsequent sneaker references. The original is never finished because culture keeps reinterpreting it.

## Mechanic
A two-minute film sampling Frank Sinatra's "My Way" with contemporary artists covering it in radically different styles — each version a different cultural reinterpretation of the same source. Images of cultural icons and Adidas Originals athletes intercut with archival and contemporary footage. The film was a deliberate provocation: it recontextualised cultural icons in ways that generated controversy and conversation.

## Why it worked
P16 (long-form drama) as cultural essay. P09 (user as co-author): the controversy around the film's recontextualisation of cultural figures generated massive organic discussion. The "My Way" hook was a formal argument for the brand idea: the song has been covered hundreds of times; each cover is an original act of interpretation. Adidas is the sneaker that culture keeps reinterpreting — which is the definition of an original.

## Steal
When your brand idea is about cultural inheritance, find a cultural artefact that demonstrates that idea structurally, not symbolically. The multiple covers of "My Way" don't illustrate the brand idea — they enact it.
