# Role Models (Hillary Clinton · 2016)

## Insight
Children absorb what adults say as normal. An opponent whose rhetoric included racial slurs, mockery of the disabled, and misogyny was not just a political problem — he was a parenting problem.

## Mechanic
A film showing children watching TV footage of the opponent's inflammatory statements — their expressions registering the words. The implicit question: what role model are we presenting? No need to argue policy; the argument is moral and parental.

## Why it worked
Long-form drama (P16): the film's power accumulates through children's reactions rather than through argument or counter-attack. Truth telling (P13): the footage is real; nothing is fabricated. The parental framing transcended political affiliation — it was an appeal to a value (what kind of person do we want our children to become?) that crossed party lines.

## Steal
When the opponent's own words are damaging, the most powerful ad is a documentary compilation of those words with the right witness. Children watching and reacting is the most credible audience proxy because they haven't yet learned to rationalise.
