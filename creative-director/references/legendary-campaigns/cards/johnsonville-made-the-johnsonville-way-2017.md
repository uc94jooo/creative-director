# Made the Johnsonville Way (Johnsonville · 2017)

## Insight
Johnsonville is a family-owned sausage company from Sheboygan, Wisconsin — genuinely weird, genuinely family-run, genuinely not corporate. In a world of fake authenticity, the company's actual strangeness was the asset. The employees are co-owners; they run the company. That's genuinely unusual.

## Mechanic
A campaign in which Johnsonville's actual employees wrote, starred in, and produced the advertising. Not "consumer-generated" content — literal employee-generated content. The films are awkward, charming, and specific in ways no advertising agency could fabricate. The production quality reflects what employees making their own ads looks like.

## Why it worked
Absurd as carrier (P11): employees acting in ads they wrote themselves is inherently funny and disarming. Long-form drama (P16): the character of the employees — their actual personalities — carries the narrative weight. The authenticity is bulletproof because it literally is authentic. The campaign works because Johnsonville actually is the thing the campaign claims it is.

## Steal
When your brand has genuine operational distinctiveness (employee ownership, unusual structure, weird founding story), let the operation be the ad. The most authentic campaign is one that only your company could make.
