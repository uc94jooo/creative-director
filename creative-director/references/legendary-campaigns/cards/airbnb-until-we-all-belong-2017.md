# Until We All Belong (Airbnb · 2017)

## Insight
In Australia, a national debate over marriage equality had reached a postal vote that excluded millions from participating in their own rights. A brand built on the idea that "belonging" is universal had an authentic stake in the outcome.

## Mechanic
Airbnb created an "Acceptance Ring" — a ring made with a deliberate gap in it, symbolising the gap in equality — and distributed it to supporters who committed to wearing it until same-sex marriage was legal. People wore and shared the rings widely. The campaign ran during the Australian Marriage Law Postal Survey period.

## Why it worked
Brand as activist (P04): Airbnb's business is fundamentally about people feeling they belong anywhere — the stance was structurally consistent with the brand idea, not opportunistic. User as co-author (P09): the ring-wearers became the campaign medium. The physical object anchored a digital conversation.

## Steal
When a brand's core purpose is directly affected by a social or legal injustice, build a physical artefact that lets supporters signal the position without shouting. Objects persist longer than hashtags and require more commitment from participants — that commitment creates emotional investment.
