# Dad Song (Old Spice · 2014)

## Insight
The companion to Mom Song. Fathers experience the same loss as their sons grow up and transition to adult grooming — but fathers express it differently, less vocally, with the same underlying emotion under a more stoic surface.

## Mechanic
A follow-up film to Mom Song, playing the same operatic absurdist conceit from the father's perspective. The tonal shift — from maternal expressiveness to paternal restraint, both played for the same comedic effect — extends the platform into new emotional territory.

## Why it worked
Absurd as carrier (P11): the formula is consistent — operatic melodrama applied to the mundane product moment — which makes the tonal variation (dad vs. mom) the new joke rather than requiring a new concept. Long-form drama (P16): the musical structure requires and justifies extended runtime. Platform thinking: the same mechanic generates multiple executions without repeating itself.

## Steal
A strong platform idea can be extended by varying one variable at a time. Mom → Dad is a simple variation that doubles your content while remaining recognizable. Don't change the concept; change the character.
