# Endless Possibilities (Getty Images · 2018)

## Insight
Stock photography has historically presented a narrow, homogeneous vision of what success, family, love, and business look like. Getty's library is vast enough to represent almost any version of human experience — but most buyers default to the clichéd images.

## Mechanic
An integrated campaign using Getty's own image library as the creative material — showing the same narrative moments (a first day of work, a wedding, a child's birthday) in wildly diverse configurations drawn from real library assets. The campaign argued that representation in visual media is a choice, and Getty enables better choices.

## Why it worked
User as co-author (P09): the audience's use of Getty images downstream was the implicit extension of the campaign — every image buyer who chose a more representative image was participating in the campaign's purpose. Design as idea (P17): the visual language was the argument, demonstrated through real images rather than described in copy.

## Steal
When your product is visual content, use the product itself as the creative material. Don't describe what your library can do — show the range of stories it contains by telling the same story multiple ways through images that already exist in the catalogue.
