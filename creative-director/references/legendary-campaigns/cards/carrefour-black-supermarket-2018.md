# Black Supermarket (Carrefour · 2018)

## Insight
EU regulations banned the sale of hundreds of heritage vegetable varieties — old seeds that had fed Europe for centuries — under standardisation rules ostensibly about consumer safety but practically protecting large agribusiness. Farmers were left with harvests they couldn't legally sell. Carrefour had the shelf space to sell them.

## Mechanic
Carrefour opened pop-up "Black Supermarket" stores selling the banned heritage varieties — tomatoes, potatoes, herbs — under signs reading "We sell what farmers can't." Simultaneously lobbied the EU to change the law. The law changed. Campaign ran across France with media coverage amplifying the activist framing.

## Why it worked
Brand as activist (P04): the action was structural — Carrefour broke EU law deliberately and publicly, using commercial infrastructure as a political act. User as co-author (P09): customers who bought the produce were participants in a lobbying campaign, not just shoppers. The three-part construction (break law + sell product + lobby to change law) gave the story a narrative arc and a victory.

## Steal
Identify a regulation that harms producers or consumers unjustly and use your distribution infrastructure to violate it openly. The most credible brand activism is the kind that risks something real. Combining commercial act + legal challenge + legislative outcome is the gold standard of campaign mechanics.
