# Blueberries (Skoda · 2009)

## Insight
Skoda's tone is warm, slightly absurdist, and self-aware. The brand doesn't take itself seriously enough to be portentous — and that restraint is itself distinctive in automotive advertising.

## Mechanic
A film in Skoda's characteristically dry, affectionate register — likely involving an incongruous domestic detail (blueberries) applied to automotive context in a way that creates gentle comedy without resolution.

## Why it worked
Skoda's tonal consistency — across multiple executions — built a recognisable brand voice that distinguished it from the aspirational posturing of competitors. Any single execution benefited from the accumulated tonal equity.

## Steal
Tonal consistency across small, domestic, affectionate executions can build more brand equity than a single expensive epic. The accumulation is the strategy.
