# Talking Stain (Tide · 2008)

## Insight
A stain at the wrong moment doesn't just embarrass you — it actively sabotages you. It talks over you, drowns you out, undermines everything you say. The stain is not just a problem; it's an adversary with an agenda.

## Mechanic
Super Bowl spot: a man in a stain-covered shirt gives a job interview. Every time he speaks, the stain itself speaks louder — gargling, babbling, completely overwhelming his words. The interviewer only hears the stain. Tide presented as the solution to this betrayal.

## Why it worked
Giving the stain a voice turned an abstract anxiety (looking unprofessional) into a vivid enemy with agency. The premise was absurd but emotionally true — a stain really does speak for you. The Super Bowl context amplified the stakes and the comedy simultaneously.

## Steal
Abstract anxieties become unforgettable when personified as antagonists with specific agendas. The stain didn't just embarrass; it actively sabotaged. Find the enemy your product eliminates and give it a personality.
