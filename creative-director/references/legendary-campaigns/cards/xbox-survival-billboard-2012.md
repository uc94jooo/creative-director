# Survival Billboard (Xbox · 2012)

## Insight
Survival games test endurance and willpower — the same skills that endurance stunt competitions test. For Halo or a survival game launch, putting real people in a genuine physical endurance challenge makes the "survival" genre claim visceral rather than virtual.

## Mechanic
A billboard on which four players competed in a live survival challenge — exposed to the elements, sustained only by the game — with the last person standing winning the ultimate gaming setup. The competition was broadcast live and online, generating earned media throughout.

## Why it worked
P09 (user as co-author): real participants, real endurance, real audience investment. P05 (cultural hijack) of the stunt competition format used to demonstrate a game mechanic. The campaign turned a product launch into a week-long spectacle that gaming culture watched actively rather than advertising that interrupted.

## Steal
If your product's core mechanic (survival, strategy, speed) can be literalised in the physical world with real people, the physical stunt is more credible and more media-efficient than any film about the product.
