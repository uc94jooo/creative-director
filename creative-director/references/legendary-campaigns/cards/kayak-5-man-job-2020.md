# 5-Man Job (KAYAK · 2020)

## Insight
Travel search should be simple, but people overcomplicate it by involving too many opinions. KAYAK's utility is speed and consolidation — it does the job better alone than through committee.

## Mechanic
An absurdist spot playing on heist-movie conventions, applying the "5-man job" archetype to travel planning — framing the search for the right flight as a multi-person operation, then undercutting it with the simplicity of KAYAK.

## Why it worked
The heist genre tropes are universally recognizable, and applying them to travel search amplifies the everyday anxiety of comparison shopping into something comic. The punchline — you don't need a crew — delivers the product benefit through contrast rather than explanation.

## Steal
Apply genre conventions from action or thriller films to mundane purchase decisions to expose the absurdity of how much energy people waste on them. The genre gap is the joke; the product is the resolution.
