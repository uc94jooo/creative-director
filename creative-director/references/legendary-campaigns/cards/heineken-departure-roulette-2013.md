# Departure Roulette (Heineken · 2013)

## Insight
Most people talk about adventure but plan safety. The fantasy of spontaneous travel — "just going somewhere, anywhere" — is real, but the actual moment of choosing it never arrives because life always offers a reason not to. Heineken's "Open Your World" platform stands for openness; the most literal form of openness is agreeing to not know where you're going.

## Mechanic
A stunt at JFK airport: travelers were asked to spin a roulette wheel that would determine their destination — immediately, with their existing luggage. If they spun it, they had to get on the plane, wherever it was going. The brand gave them money, a Heineken, and filmed what happened.

## Why it worked
The stunt is a genuine test of the brand's stated value. People who accepted were living the "Open Your World" philosophy in real time, not in a film about living it. The resulting footage is more credible than any scripted adventure narrative because the risk was real. The brand put money into a social experiment rather than a production budget.

## Steal
When your brand stands for a value that most people endorse but few practice, create a situation that makes the gap between endorsement and practice visible — and then give people the opportunity to close it. The ones who do become your most credible advocates.
