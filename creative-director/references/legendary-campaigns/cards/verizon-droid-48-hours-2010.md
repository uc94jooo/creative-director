# 48 Hours (Verizon Droid · 2010)

## Insight
TODO: Verify year. Part of Verizon's Droid campaign era — positioning Android against iPhone. A battery life or capability demonstration using 48-hour continuous use as the proof point.

## Mechanic
An absurdist spot using 48 hours of continuous use as the premise — likely showing increasingly extreme scenarios over a 48-hour period where the Droid remains functional, contrasted against implicitly failing competitors.

## Why it worked
Battery life and durability as product claims are unconvincing when stated but compelling when demonstrated through extreme use scenarios. The 48-hour framing gave specificity that "all-day battery" lacked.

## Steal
Vague durability claims ("long-lasting," "reliable") become memorable when given a specific time frame and an absurd use case. The specificity of "48 hours" is more believable than an unquantified claim.
