# Bookbook (IKEA · 2014)

## Insight
The physical IKEA catalogue was considered old-fashioned in a digital era — seen as a liability rather than an asset.

## Mechanic
A film parodying Apple product launch videos, presenting the IKEA print catalogue as a revolutionary new technology: "eternal battery life," "intuitive touch interface," "no lag," "tactile haptics." The joke is that all Apple's language applies literally to a paper catalogue.

## Why it worked
By treating the product's "weakness" (being non-digital) as its defining innovation, IKEA inverted the cultural hierarchy between print and digital. The Apple parody borrowed Apple's cultural authority and reflected it back with irony. The catalogue became shareable content rather than junk mail.

## Steal
Reframe an analogue or old-fashioned product feature using the aspirational language of a high-status adjacent category. The contrast between the grandiose language and the simple object is the joke — and the argument.
