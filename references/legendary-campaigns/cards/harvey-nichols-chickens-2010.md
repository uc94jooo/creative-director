# Chickens (Harvey Nichols · 2010)

## Insight
Sale periods at luxury retailers create an undignified scramble entirely at odds with the brand's exclusivity positioning — customers behave like panicked animals.

## Mechanic
Outdoor posters showing headless chickens with Harvey Nichols branding for the sale period — a self-deprecating joke about the frenzy the sale creates while maintaining the brand's ironic, self-aware tone.

## Why it worked
Luxury advertising that can laugh at its own sale-period chaos feels more confident than brands that pretend the exclusivity is maintained even during discount. The absurd image is memorable because it is true and unexpected from a prestige retailer.

## Steal
Find the moment your brand's behaviour most visibly contradicts its positioning and beat the audience to the joke. Self-deprecation about real contradictions reads as confidence, not weakness.
