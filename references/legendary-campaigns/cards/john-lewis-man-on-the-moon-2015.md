# Man on the Moon (John Lewis · 2015)

## Insight
Christmas intensifies loneliness for older people — the season most associated with connection is the season when isolation is most acute, yet no retailer had ever addressed this directly.

## Mechanic
A film where a young girl spots an elderly man alone on the moon through her telescope. She attempts to communicate; eventually sends him a wrapped gift that reaches him on Christmas Day. Partnership with Age UK activating around elderly loneliness.

## Why it worked
John Lewis addressed the social taboo of Christmas loneliness from within Christmas advertising — the category that most avoids it. The imaginative framing (moon, telescope, gift-by-balloon) made the sadness bearable rather than crushing, while the Age UK partnership gave the audience a real action to take.

## Steal
Social cause + imaginative framing + real partner activation = the full loop: emotion → awareness → action. Any one element alone is weaker. Together they make the brand a participant in the problem, not just its describer.
