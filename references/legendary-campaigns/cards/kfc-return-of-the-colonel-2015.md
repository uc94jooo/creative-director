# The Return of the Colonel (KFC · 2015)

## Insight
KFC had become a brand living off a dead founder's legacy without acknowledging it. The Colonel was everywhere — on every bucket, every sign — but nowhere in the brand's actual communication. The gap between heritage and behaviour was the comedy.

## Mechanic
KFC reintroduced Colonel Sanders as an active character played by rotating celebrities (Darrell Hammond, Norm Macdonald, Jim Gaffigan, Rob Lowe, Reba McEntire, and others), each delivering a distinct comedic persona. The absurdist campaign had no continuity between performers — the same character played by wildly different people was the joke.

## Why it worked
P11 (absurd as carrier) built around the structural irony of a brand icon played by rotating strangers. P05 (cultural hijack) borrowed the fame of unexpected celebrity choices — each casting was itself a PR moment. The campaign self-consciously made the Colonel's unreality the content, which paradoxically made him more present in culture than the previous decade of passive logo usage.

## Steal
If your brand already has an icon it doesn't use, reactivating it ironically — acknowledging the absurdity of the icon itself — is more honest and more entertaining than treating legacy seriously. The meta-awareness is the differentiator.
