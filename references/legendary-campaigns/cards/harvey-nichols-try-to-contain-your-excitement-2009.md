# Try to Contain Your Excitement (Harvey Nichols · 2009)

## Insight
British understatement applied to luxury shopping — the politely suppressed excitement of the Harvey Nichols customer is more distinctive than overt enthusiasm.

## Mechanic
Outdoor posters using the driest possible copywriting to describe items that would typically warrant excitement — communicating the brand's ironic, self-aware British tone through deliberate anti-climax.

## Why it worked
The tonal gap between the quality of the product and the deadpan copy signals cultural sophistication. Understatement is a class marker; the customer who gets it feels identified as "the right type."

## Steal
Express maximum product quality through minimum emotional register. The understatement signals that the audience is too sophisticated to need enthusiasm explained to them.
