# Tipping Point (Guinness · 2007)

## Insight

The moment something tips from potential to kinetic energy — from stillness to cascade — is the most thrilling moment in any system. Guinness understood that their pint-pour ritual is itself a tipping point: the moment anticipation becomes reality.

## Mechanic

A 90-second TV film directed by Nicolai Fuglsig (MJZ): a massive domino-chain of fridge-sized dominoes, bouncy balls, tires, and paint canisters cascading through a fictional South American village. The chain ultimately triggers a Guinness beer glass filling. Filmed in Argentina with a crew of hundreds. The cascade took four days to set up for each take.

## Why it worked

P08 (craft as message) at its peak: the sheer logistics of the shoot — real objects, real physics, real people — transferred directly to a sense of craftsmanship about the brand. P16 (long-form drama) used the mechanics of anticipation (will the chain complete?) to mirror the Guinness pour ritual. The film earned its length because every second advanced a genuine physical question.

## Steal

Rube Goldberg logic applied at cinematic scale: find the chain of causality in your product's benefit, then build it as literally and physically as possible. The audience's anxiety about whether the chain will complete becomes the advertising.
