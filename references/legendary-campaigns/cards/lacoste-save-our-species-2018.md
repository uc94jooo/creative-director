# Save Our Species (Lacoste · 2018)

## Insight
Lacoste's crocodile logo — one of fashion's most recognisable icons — is a species itself. Ten endangered species had smaller surviving populations than Lacoste had produced polo shirts that season.

## Mechanic
For a limited edition collection, Lacoste replaced its iconic crocodile logo with ten endangered species — each species' representation proportional to its surviving wild population. The rarest species had the fewest polo shirts made. Sold through IUCN partnership. Sold out immediately.

## Why it worked
Design as idea (P17): the logo replacement was the entire campaign. No film, no headline could do what the visual substitution accomplished in a single glance. Brand as activist (P04): the sacrifice was real — Lacoste surrendered its most valuable asset (the logo) to make the point credible.

## Steal
When a brand's most iconic visual element can become a medium for a message, the act of substituting it is more powerful than any communication built around it. The willingness to give up the logo for a purpose signals genuine commitment over stated values.
