# Field Trip to Mars (Lockheed Martin · 2016)

## Insight
Lockheed Martin is building the technology to take humans to Mars — but the people who most need to understand and support that mission are not the current generation of voters and taxpayers; they are the children who will live in that world. The most effective case for a Mars mission is making children feel they are already going.

## Mechanic
A school bus fitted with augmented reality — transparent screens replacing the windows — that transformed a normal school field trip into a simulated journey to Mars. As the bus drove through Washington DC, the windows showed a photorealistic Martian landscape synced to the bus's actual movement. Children experienced Mars as a real place without leaving the city.

## Why it worked
P18 (tech as canvas): the technology is not a demonstration but the experience itself. P09 (user as co-author): children became participants in a journey rather than audience for an advertisement. The Cannes Grand Prix validated that B2B brands can earn attention through human experiences that have nothing to do with product features. Lockheed Martin did not explain its Mars mission; it made children feel it.

## Steal
The most credible argument for a future technology is making people experience it in the present. If your brand is building something that doesn't exist yet, find the closest possible present-tense simulation of that future and let people live in it briefly.
