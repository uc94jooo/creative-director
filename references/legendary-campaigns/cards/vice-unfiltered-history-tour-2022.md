# Unfiltered History Tour (VICE · 2022)

## Insight
The British Museum holds thousands of artefacts removed from colonised countries — a permanent, unchallenged historical injustice displayed as culture. The museum's own audio guides present only the coloniser's narrative.

## Mechanic
An unofficial AR audio tour of the British Museum: visitors pointed their phones at specific exhibits and received alternative narrations — voices of historians and descendants from the countries of origin (India, Egypt, Nigeria, etc.) telling the real history of each object's acquisition. Distributed as an Instagram filter, bypassing the institution entirely.

## Why it worked
Technology as canvas (P18): AR made an illegal counter-narrative possible inside the museum itself without physical confrontation. Truth-telling (P13): the alternative histories were rigorously documented, not polemic. The institution's own space became the medium for its critique.

## Steal
When an institution controls the official narrative, use technology to distribute an unauthorised counter-narrative through the same physical space. The institution's legitimacy becomes the backdrop for the critique rather than a barrier to it.
