# Childlike Imagination (GE · 2014)

## Insight
A child who grows up to work at GE sees the company as her parent did — through a lens of limitless possibility. The gap between "what GE actually does" and "what a child thinks GE does" is smaller than most adults would assume, because GE genuinely does work on things that seem fantastical.

## Mechanic
A long-form film following a young girl who describes her mother's work at GE to other children through the lens of imagination — flying machines, magical engines, medical miracles — and the reveal being that she's not wrong. The industrial reality is as wondrous as the childlike description.

## Why it worked
Long-form drama (P16) gave room for the emotional arc between innocence and industrial truth to land with full weight. The film reframes GE's industrial work as genuinely wonder-worthy — not through a claim but through the perspective of someone for whom wonder is the default.

## Steal
Use a child's literal misunderstanding of a complex product or company as a creative frame. When the child's "wrong" description is actually more accurate than the corporate version, the audience experiences a reframe that works better than any tagline.
