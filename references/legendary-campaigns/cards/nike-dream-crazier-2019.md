# Dream Crazier (Nike · 2019)

## Insight
Every adjective used to dismiss women in sport — hysterical, dramatic, delusional — maps exactly to the behaviours celebrated in men: passion, intensity, ambition. The word "crazy" is not an insult; it is a cover story for what the culture cannot accept in women.

## Mechanic
A 90-second film narrated by Serena Williams, debuted during the Oscars, featuring historic and contemporary footage of female athletes being told they're "too emotional," "too aggressive," or "too much." The film inverts each dismissal into a badge of honour, concluding: "It's only crazy until you do it. Just Do It."

## Why it worked
"Dream Crazy" established a platform; "Dream Crazier" made it specific to women without repeating the argument — it extended the insight into a new territory with equal sharpness. P13 (truth-telling) named the language of dismissal precisely, not generally. Serena Williams as narrator was not celebrity endorsement but proof — the most dismissed champion in the sport became the voice of the claim.

## Steal
When a platform lands, the second execution must be more specific, not broader. Extension means taking the original insight and finding the sharpest manifestation in a new audience segment — not diluting the insight to include more people.
