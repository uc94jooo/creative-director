# Brewtroleum (DB Export · 2015)

## Insight
DB Export had positioned itself as a "lighter" beer but lacked a differentiating proof. The spent grain from brewing — typically waste — can be converted into biofuel. The byproduct of drinking beer could literally fuel New Zealand.

## Mechanic
DB partnered with Green Biologics to convert spent grain from DB Export brewing into a biofuel blended into regular petrol — Brewtroleum. The fuel was sold at Z Energy stations. For every DB Export bottle sold, a small contribution went toward producing more Brewtroleum. The product was real; the circle was real.

## Why it worked
Absurdism (P11): "Saving the planet by drinking beer" is a fundamentally absurd and hilarious premise. Product as proof (P14): the biofuel actually existed and worked — this wasn't a claim, it was a demonstration of a real closed loop. The combination of absurd premise and genuine proof made the campaign impossible to dismiss.

## Steal
When a brand's waste or byproduct can be converted into something of value, the act of conversion is more powerful than any environmental claim. Real closed loops are better arguments than stated sustainability commitments.
