# A Piece of Me (KPN · 2024)

## Insight
One in 100 teenagers becomes a victim of online image-based shaming — the non-consensual sharing of intimate images. The cultural conversation defaults to blaming victims. A Dutch telecom company that wants the internet to be safe for everyone has both the moral standing and the unique cultural reach to shift that conversation — but only if it gives up the ad format entirely and enters the cultural space where Gen Z actually lives: music.

## Mechanic
KPN collaborated with the Netherlands' most prominent singer-songwriter to write and produce an original song and music video — "A Piece of Me" — built from real accounts of image-based abuse victims. The song didn't advertise KPN; it shifted cultural blame from victims to perpetrators. Within a week it trended on YouTube and Spotify, surpassing global artists. It sparked the largest national debate on online shaming ever seen in the Netherlands, entered parliamentary debate, and one month after release a new law passed making non-consensual image sharing part of the Sexual Crimes Act. Hundreds of schools now use the music video as classroom curriculum. A UK version followed, turning a Dutch campaign into a global mission.

## Why it worked
P07 (Utility as Brand): KPN's actual service commitment to internet safety gave them standing to fund a cultural intervention rather than an awareness campaign. P17 (Cause as Core): the song became the campaign — no KPN branding in the music video, only cultural impact. The decision to make a real song by a real artist, not a branded content piece, was what allowed it to function as genuine culture. Parliamentary change and curriculum adoption are outcomes advertising almost never achieves.

## Steal
When a brand's purpose intersects with a legal or social injustice, the most powerful execution is to fund an artist to make real culture, not to make an ad about the cause. Real music charts; branded purpose content does not. The brand's name can be absent from the work entirely if the work itself is funded by the brand.
