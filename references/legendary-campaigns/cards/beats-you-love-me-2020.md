# You Love Me (Beats by Dre · 2020)

## Insight
Black America was simultaneously at peak cultural visibility and peak existential danger in summer 2020. A brand that built its entire identity on Black culture could not stay neutral — but generic solidarity statements had already flooded the market.

## Mechanic
A manifest film narrated by Tiffany Haddish, cutting through decades of Black cultural contributions — music, fashion, sport, language — with the provocation: "You love Black culture. Do you love Black people?" Not an apology, not a promise: a statement.

## Why it worked
While every brand was publishing "we stand with you" statements, Beats inverted the dynamic: not asking for acceptance but confronting the audience with a debt already owed. The tone was accusatory and earned — Beats had genuine standing as a Black-founded brand. The accusation read as truth, not performance.

## Steal
In moments of social tension, "we are with you" is the most common and least credible brand response. The alternative: state the contradiction the audience already knows but hasn't heard said out loud. Constating is more powerful than declaring solidarity.
