# Monty the Penguin (John Lewis · 2014)

## Insight
Children form profound attachments to imaginary companions — and parents buy gifts for those companions as earnestly as for their child.

## Mechanic
A film following a boy and his penguin friend Monty through the year. The boy notices Monty is lonely and gives him what he needs for Christmas — a penguin companion. Reveal: both penguins are stuffed toys, the gift the boy received.

## Why it worked
The dual reveal (companion is imagined; gift was the toy) lands as a double emotional punch. The partnership with WWF and the sale of Monty toys made the film commercially executable beyond awareness. The film established a character — Monty — who audiences formed attachments to independently.

## Steal
Build an emotionally charged long-form story around a character strong enough to be merchandised. When the character is loveable before the product appears, the product inherits the love.
