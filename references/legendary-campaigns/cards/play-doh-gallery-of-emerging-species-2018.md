# The Gallery of Emerging Species (Play-Doh · 2018)

## Insight
Children's imagination produces genuinely alien, absurdist creatures — neither fish nor fowl, belonging to no known taxonomy. Play-Doh's product is not a toy; it is a world-building material.

## Mechanic
Play-Doh collected children's creations and exhibited them in a real gallery framed as an exhibition of "emerging species" — complete with pseudo-scientific naming and taxonomy plaques. The absurd seriousness of the gallery format amplifies the joy of the creatures.

## Why it worked
The gallery context elevates children's work to art without condescension. The absurdist taxonomy makes adults laugh while validating children. The format — a real gallery, not a digital showcase — creates earned media and a genuine artifact worth visiting.

## Steal
Take your audience's output seriously enough to give it an institutional frame. The gap between the grandeur of the frame and the playfulness of the content IS the idea.
