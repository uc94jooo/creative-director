# SFW XXX (Diesel · 2010)

## Insight
Fashion advertising had become indistinguishable through sexualization — every brand used provocative imagery to signal aspiration. The codes of sexy were so overused they had become invisible. If everyone is provocative, no one is provocative.

## Mechanic
Diesel created a campaign branded "SFW XXX" (Safe For Work XXX) — explicit-sounding but deliberately absurd and non-explicit imagery. The campaign used the grammar of pornographic advertising (titles, framing, staging conventions) but substituted completely banal or comedic content for anything actually sexual. A series of films and print ads carried the parody.

## Why it worked
P03 (behavior inversion): when every competitor uses explicit sexuality, using the signals of explicit sexuality without the content is a more powerful attention device than actual explicitness. The parody of the category's codes exposed how mechanical and empty those codes had become. P11 (absurdism): the deadpan commitment to the "SFW XXX" format generated the humor and the intrigue simultaneously. Diesel positioned itself as self-aware enough to mock the conventions it could have deployed sincerely.

## Steal
In categories saturated with a specific content type (sex, aspirational lifestyle, technology), the most distinctive move is to parody those conventions with complete commitment. The parody works only when it's entirely earnest in execution — winking breaks the spell.
