## Mechanic
An absurdist spot using telephone communication as the setup — likely a game of telephone gone surreal, or a phone conversation interrupted by impossible Skittles-related transformations. The deadpan commitment to an illogical premise is the Skittles creative signature.

## Why it worked
Phone calls provide a two-party structure that absurdist comedy can exploit — the gap between what one party says and what the other hears, or the impossibility of communicating the absurd via a normal channel. Skittles used mundane communication formats to make the absurdity feel grounded.

## Steal
Use the structure of everyday communication (phone call, letter, text) as the frame for impossible events. The banality of the medium contrasts with the absurdity of the content in a way that amplifies both.
