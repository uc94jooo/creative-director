# Google Home of the Whopper (Burger King · 2017)

## Insight
Burger King had 15 seconds in a TV ad — not enough time to describe all the Whopper's ingredients. Google Home devices, sitting in millions of living rooms, could read out the entire Wikipedia entry for the Whopper if triggered by a voice command. The audience was already equipped with the solution.

## Mechanic
A Burger King TV ad ended with an actor saying "OK Google, what is the Whopper burger?" — triggering Google Home devices in viewers' homes to read the Whopper's Wikipedia page aloud. Burger King then edited the Whopper's Wikipedia page to be more promotional. Chaos and coverage followed.

## Why it worked
Format as idea (P01): the 15-second TV ad is structurally incomplete without the Google Home device. They are one ad together. The audacity of activating a competitor's hardware as your own advertising medium generated global coverage. The Wikipedia manipulation added a second act.

## Steal
Audit every connected device in your audience's home or pocket. Find the one that, if triggered by your ad, would extend your message into their space without media spend. Then trigger it.
