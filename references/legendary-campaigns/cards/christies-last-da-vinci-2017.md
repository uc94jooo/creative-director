# The Last Da Vinci (Christie's · 2017)

## Insight
The Salvator Mundi — attributed to Leonardo da Vinci — was the rarest object on Earth: the last privately owned Leonardo, lost for centuries and rediscovered. The auction was not just a sale; it was a cultural event that the entire world had a stake in.

## Mechanic
A global media and events campaign built around the discovery, authentication, and auctioning of the Salvator Mundi. Cultural hijack (P05): the painting's story was so extraordinary that Christie's role was to frame and amplify it rather than create anything. User as co-author (P09): public exhibition tours, documentary films, and digital engagement made the auction a participatory cultural moment.

## Why it worked
The object's cultural power was so immense that the campaign's job was curation rather than creation. Cultural hijack (P05): Christie's attached itself to a story that was already world news. The $450 million final price was the ultimate proof of concept — though subsequent questions about authentication added complexity to the legacy.

## Steal
When you have access to a genuinely unique or historic object, product, or story, the campaign's job is to get out of the way and let the object do the work. Curation > creation. Give the world access to something extraordinary and they will do your marketing for you.
