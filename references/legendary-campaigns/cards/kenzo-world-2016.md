# Kenzo World (Kenzo · 2016)

## Insight
Luxury fragrance advertising is uniformly the same: beautiful people, slow movement, inexplicable scenarios, earnestness. The audience is sophisticated enough to recognise the category codes and feel imprisoned by them.

## Mechanic
Spike Jonze directs Margaret Qualley escaping a boring gala through a five-minute unbroken dance sequence of escalating physical extremity and absurdist joy. No fragrance shown until the final seconds. No romance. No conventional beauty signalling. Pure kinetic liberation.

## Why it worked
The film is genuinely worth watching independent of the brand. The invisible brand strategy (P07) earns trust; the extraordinary craft (P08) earns attention; the long form (P16) earns the emotion. The brand appears only when the release is earned. The anti-convention of the whole film signals what the brand is by showing what it is not.

## Steal
When your category is locked into a visual convention, commission a director known outside advertising. Give them the brief, not the execution. The result will look like their work — and nothing like the category.
