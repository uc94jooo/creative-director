# Stuck (Thorntons · 2009)

## Insight
Chocolate as an indulgence is typically marketed through sensory pleasure — slow melting, beautiful photography, gratification. Thorntons' positioning allowed for a different angle: the compulsive, uncontrollable pleasure of chocolate, taken to an absurd extreme.

## Mechanic
A Harmony Korine-directed spot (the director of "Kids," "Spring Breakers") — bringing arthouse sensibility to a confectionery brand. "Stuck" likely refers to a physical or emotional state of being unable to stop consuming, literalized through absurdist or surreal imagery. The auteur director choice signals that Thorntons was willing to make something genuinely unusual for the category.

## Why it worked
Hiring Harmony Korine to direct a chocolate ad is itself a P11 absurdist move — the mismatch between director and product creates intrigue before a single frame is seen. The long-form format (P16 element) allowed for the kind of unsettling, non-commercial visual grammar that Korine specializes in, which would be impossible in a 30-second format.

## Steal
Director-as-message: the choice of creative collaborator is sometimes the most significant creative decision. Hiring a director whose visual language is incompatible with the category creates friction that generates attention and distinctiveness.
