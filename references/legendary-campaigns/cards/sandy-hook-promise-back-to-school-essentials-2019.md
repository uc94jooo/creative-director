# Back-to-School Essentials (Sandy Hook Promise · 2019)

## Insight
"Back to school" is the most relentlessly commercial and optimistic event in American culture — a ritual of new starts, new supplies, bright futures. The same objects children pack in their bags are the objects they use to survive a school shooting.

## Mechanic
A film framed as a cheerful back-to-school supply advertisement — children showing off their new sneakers, their notebooks, their backpacks. The voiceover is warm and retail-friendly. But each object is then demonstrated in its other use: the sneakers for running from a shooter, the socks as a tourniquet, the backpack held up as a shield, the scissors as a weapon of self-defence. It ends with the Sandy Hook Promise hotline.

## Why it worked
Behaviour inversion (P03) at maximum severity: the most positive cultural code (back-to-school) is rewritten with the most horrific context (school shooting survival). The contrast is so violent that the audience cannot process it without experiencing the point physically. Truth-telling (P13): every use demonstrated is real — genuinely what students are taught in active shooter drills.

## Steal
Take the most joyful, commercial, category-coded moment in your cultural calendar and populate it with the darkest possible adjacent reality. The contrast is the argument. The joy makes the darkness more unbearable, not less.
