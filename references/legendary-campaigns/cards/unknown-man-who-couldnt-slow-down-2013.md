# The Man Who Couldn't Slow Down (unknown · 2013)

## Insight
TODO: Verify brand. The film explores a character archetype familiar to the financial services audience — a high-achiever who cannot stop, cannot retire, cannot slow down — and uses this as an entry point into a conversation about life planning and financial wellbeing.

## Mechanic
A long-form film following a protagonist defined by relentless forward motion — professionally successful but emotionally stranded — whose arc invites reflection on what wealth is actually for.

## Why it worked
Long-form drama (P16): the character study requires extended runtime to earn the emotional weight of the insight. Truth telling (P13): the archetype is recognizable to the target audience — successful people who have not asked the bigger questions. Finance advertising that sells meaning rather than products.

## Steal
In financial services, the most effective insight is not about money management but about what money cannot manage. The character who has everything except an answer to "what now?" is a universal and underused protagonist for this category.
