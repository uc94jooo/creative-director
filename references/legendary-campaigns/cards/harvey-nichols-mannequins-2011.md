# Mannequins (Harvey Nichols · 2011)

## Insight
Harvey Nichols' sale prices are so low they feel shocking even to store mannequins — the discount is dramatic enough to warrant a visible reaction.

## Mechanic
Window display and print showing mannequins in postures of apparent shock or distress at the sale prices being shown — using the physical retail display format as comedy performance.

## Why it worked
Animating inanimate store fixtures as emotional responders to the brand's own sale communicated dramatic discount in an unexpected, memorable visual format. The joke required no copy — the posture told the story.

## Steal
Use fixed, physical brand assets (mannequins, signage, product packaging) as characters responding to your own brand message. Giving inert objects an emotional reaction costs nothing and breaks visual convention.
