# Shoplifters (Harvey Nichols · 2013)

## Insight
Luxury shopping is driven by desire so intense it bypasses normal inhibition — Harvey Nichols shoppers would rather steal than go without.

## Mechanic
A film showing people committing increasingly absurd acts of petty crime — pickpocketing, stealing from coworkers, taking from family — then cutting to: they were doing it to fund their Harvey Nichols purchases. "Love it enough to steal it."

## Why it worked
The campaign amplified the brand's aspiration rather than moderating it. Luxury advertising typically flatters; this one made desire appear slightly criminal — which made it feel more real, exclusive, and exciting.

## Steal
Take the uncomfortable implication of intense brand desire — what people would actually do to afford or own it — and make it the premise. Dark desire is more aspirational than polished aspiration.
