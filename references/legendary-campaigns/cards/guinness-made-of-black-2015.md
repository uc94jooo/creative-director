# Made of Black (Guinness · 2015)

## Insight
Guinness's defining visual characteristic — its darkness — was never meaningfully connected to its African and Black diaspora audience in a way that felt owned by them rather than assigned to them.

## Mechanic
A cinematic manifesto film celebrating Black youth culture, creativity, and identity across Nigeria and Africa, using Guinness's blackness as a metaphor for the depth, complexity, and strength of the audience's own identity. Long-form, high production, culturally embedded.

## Why it worked
Truth-telling (P13): articulating an identity pride with specificity to an audience that had not seen itself reflected in the brand at this register. Long-form drama (P16): the scope of the film gave the identity claim room to feel earned rather than sloganeered. The product metaphor (black = depth, character) connected brand attribute to human attribute without feeling forced.

## Steal
When a product's most obvious visual or physical characteristic can be reframed as a metaphor for the audience's own self-image, the brand attribute becomes an identity statement rather than a product fact.
