# Voice 2 Diabetes (KVI Brave Fund · 2024)

## Insight
Type 2 diabetes is the second-largest cause of preventable death in India, yet 50% of cases remain undiagnosed — not because screening is impossible, but because the equipment and access required puts it out of reach for billions of people. A voice sample taken on a smartphone contains detectable markers for diabetes. If you can identify the disease from sound alone, the 1 billion people who own phones already carry the screening device.

## Mechanic
KVI Brave Fund funded the development of an AI algorithm trained on over 18,000 voice recordings to detect type 2 diabetes from vocal biomarkers — specifically pitch, jitter, and shimmer patterns that differ between diabetic and non-diabetic voices. The study, published in a peer-reviewed journal in October 2023, achieved 89% accuracy for women and 86% for men. The algorithm was designed with gender equity built in: separate models for male and female voices ensure equitable accuracy across both. The tool requires only a smartphone and a brief voice sample, no blood tests or clinical equipment. Currently in beta testing and generating hospital partnerships, with seven additional disease states under study.

## Why it worked
P07 (Utility as Brand): the campaign IS the product — a healthcare tool that exists to save lives in communities of color and the developing world where traditional diagnostics cannot reach. P12 (Data Made Human): 18,000 voice recordings and acoustic biomarker data were translated into a technology with a human story — the person speaking into a phone who doesn't know they have diabetes. The 89% accuracy figure is the kind of data point that generates 4 billion media impressions because it sounds impossible until you understand it.

## Steal
When access is the problem, look for a diagnostic or service that can be delivered through infrastructure people already have. The smartphone is in 1 billion hands. The voice is free. Any screening that can be reduced to audio analysis has universal reach potential.
