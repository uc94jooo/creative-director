# Decode Jay-Z (Bing · 2010)

## Insight
Search is only meaningful when you're looking for something that matters to you. Jay-Z's autobiography was a cultural event — hiding its pages across the world turned Bing's search capability into the tool you needed to find something you genuinely wanted.

## Mechanic
Pages of Jay-Z's memoir "Decoded" were hidden in real locations around the world — on pizza boxes, pool table felt, billboard-sized prints in Miami — each photographed and released as a Bing search challenge. Find the location using Bing, win a prize. The city clues matched content in the book. A scavenger hunt using real geography as media.

## Why it worked
Every element served dual duty: the book content provided the clues, the real-world locations created photography, Bing provided discovery, and users did the sharing. The campaign demonstrated Bing's search capability through desire, not instruction. You searched because you wanted the next page, not because Bing asked you to.

## Steal
The most powerful product demonstration is one where the user never notices they're being demonstrated to. Engineer a situation where someone pursues a goal they already have, and your product is the only route to it.
