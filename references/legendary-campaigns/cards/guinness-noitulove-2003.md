# Noitulove (Guinness · 2003)

## Insight
Guinness's depth — its darkness, its complexity, its unhurried pour — connects it to something ancient, not fashionable. Running evolution backwards reveals that patience and depth are primordial, not modern, virtues.

## Mechanic
A film running human evolution in reverse — from modern bar to prehistoric sea creatures — scored to an original composition. The final shot: a pint of Guinness being poured. "Guinness. Good things come to those who wait." Three minutes of reverse time as proof of the brand's central claim.

## Why it worked
The reverse-time structure was not a trick but an argument: good things — Guinness, human complexity — took a long time to develop and are worth the wait. The craft of the reverse animation gave the claim physical weight. You don't argue with three minutes of evidence.

## Steal
When your brand benefit is patience or depth, dramatise the time investment rather than the reward. Show the cost of great things, not just the greatness. The audience respects what took effort.
