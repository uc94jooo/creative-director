# Mountain (PlayStation · 2003)

## Insight
PlayStation's games allow players to inhabit worlds and situations beyond normal human experience. A campaign that communicates the scale of those possibilities — the epic, the overwhelming, the unreachable — would do more than product features ever could.

## Mechanic
A long-form cinematic film featuring a vast mountain of human figures — potentially thousands of intertwined bodies — as a visual metaphor for the accumulated humanity inside PlayStation's gaming worlds. Epic scale without explanation.

## Why it worked
PlayStation's "Double Life" era established a brand voice of philosophical scale — gaming as a different relationship with reality. Mountain extends that territory through visual spectacle (P16 long-form craft) that communicates the impossible, epic scale of gaming experience without showing a single game. The sublime terror (overwhelming scale + wonder) is the brand emotion.

## Steal
Abstract product experiences (gaming, music, travel) can be communicated through visual scale rather than product representation. If the experience is vast, make the communication vast.
