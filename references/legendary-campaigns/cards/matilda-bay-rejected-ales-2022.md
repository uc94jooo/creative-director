# Rejected Ales (Matilda Bay · 2022)

## Insight
Craft beer enthusiasts have strong opinions — they argue passionately about what beers should exist. What if rejected internal brewery recipes were given to the public to debate and vote on?

## Mechanic
Matilda Bay released beers that had been rejected by their own brewing team, inviting consumers to taste and vote on whether the internal rejection was correct, making the audience the quality-control panel.

## Why it worked
The mechanic gave the most opinionated craft beer audience real power — not performative co-creation but actual influence over what gets made. The absurdity of releasing rejected products created conversation around the quality of the accepted ones.

## Steal
Give your most critical audience access to what you normally hide — the failures, the rejects, the near-misses. Transparency about the editorial process builds more trust than polished presentation alone.
