# The Bear (Canal+ · 2011)

## Insight
Subscribers to a premium TV channel are not buying content — they are buying the experience of people who understand film. The people who work at Canal+ care about cinema the way film students do: obsessively, technically, and with enormous self-awareness about genre and craft.

## Mechanic
A two-minute film shot as a perfectly executed nature documentary. A bear is observed in the wild — eating salmon, playing with cubs. We follow the bear with reverence and curiosity. In the final act, the bear removes a costume. He is a film director. The nature documentary was his bear suit. Canal+'s tagline: "Committed to Cinema." One of the most awarded spots of 2011.

## Why it worked
P16 (long-form drama): the nature documentary format is sustained with absolute commitment — no winking, no breaks in style — until the reveal. P11 (absurd as carrier): the absurdist reveal (the bear is a director) lands harder because the preceding two minutes were played completely straight. It also communicates the brand message with zero claims: Canal+ is a brand made by people who love film so much they will spend two minutes making a technically perfect nature documentary to say so.

## Steal
Commit to the format you are subverting until the last possible moment. The absurd reveal only works when the audience has fully surrendered to the fake premise. Any hint of self-awareness before the reveal deflates the punch.
