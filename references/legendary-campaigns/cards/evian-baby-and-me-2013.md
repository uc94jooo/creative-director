# Baby & Me (Evian · 2013)

## Insight
The third iteration of Evian's "live young" baby series required a new mechanic while remaining true to the platform. The insight: the relationship between an adult and their inner baby is not just reflection — it is interaction. They can dance together.

## Mechanic
A film in which adults encounter their baby selves — in reflections, on the other side of windows — and begin a synchronized dance sequence. Set to Ini Kamoze's "Here Comes the Hotstepper" (remixed). The baby movements match the adults' movements, creating a mirror dance between present self and younger self. 60 million YouTube views in two weeks.

## Why it worked
P11 (absurd as carrier): the synchronized baby dance escalates from cute to euphoric without losing the brand idea. P16 (long-form drama): the emotional arc builds — a single moment of wonder becomes a city-wide dance sequence. This was Evian's most shared spot and demonstrated that the "live young" platform could sustain three iterations without diminishing returns when each iteration found a genuinely new expression.

## Steal
A strong platform can sustain multiple campaigns if each execution finds a new emotional angle on the same insight. Baby & Me is not Roller Babies with a new song — it adds the concept of relationship (the adult and baby dancing together) that was absent from the earlier spots.
