# Small Business Saturday (American Express · 2010)

## Insight
Black Friday and Cyber Monday had colonized the post-Thanksgiving shopping calendar, funneling billions to large retailers and e-commerce platforms. Small businesses — the backbone of local communities — had no equivalent cultural moment. American Express cards were accepted more often at small businesses than at chain retailers; AmEx's business interest aligned with small business survival.

## Mechanic
AmEx invented a new national shopping holiday: the Saturday between Black Friday and Cyber Monday. The idea: shop local that day. AmEx provided free Facebook advertising to small businesses, created shop-local maps, and drove the hashtag. The campaign grew from a single-year stunt into an officially recognized national day — US Senate passed a resolution supporting it. Over $5.5B was spent at small businesses on the day in its peak years.

## Why it worked
AmEx didn't create an ad — it created a calendar slot. P04 (brand as activist): the sacrifice was real: AmEx funded competitor-free advertising for small merchants and positioned itself against the large retailers where AmEx cards are also accepted. P10 (serialization): the genius was creating something annual and repeatable, with growing institutional recognition. The Senate resolution was the credibility that advertising money could never buy.

## Steal
When you cannot out-spend competitors for existing cultural moments, invent one. Find the gap in the cultural calendar that serves a constituency your brand authentically represents. Design it to be legible in three words, repeatable annually, and genuinely useful to the community — not just promotionally useful to the brand.
