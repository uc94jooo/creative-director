# Close to Home (AT&T · 2018)

## Insight
Texting while driving kills 3,477 people annually in the US. The abstract statistic doesn't move people — but the specific, named human being who died because someone couldn't wait does.

## Mechanic
A long-form documentary-style film series featuring real victims of distracted driving — and the drivers who caused the accidents. Both sides share their stories without dramatization: the person who died, the person who survived them, the driver who lives with what they did.

## Why it worked
AT&T had run distracted driving campaigns for years. This iteration moved from statistics and reconstructed scenarios to real people in real aftermath. Long-form drama (P16) gave enough time for guilt, grief, and responsibility to accumulate without simplification. Truth-telling (P13): showing the driver as a person who made an ordinary mistake with irreversible consequences was more disturbing than any villain framing.

## Steal
In behaviour change campaigns, guilt is more powerful when it's specific and human than when it's abstract and statistical. Showing the person whose text could not wait — and what happened — creates a mirror the audience cannot dismiss.
