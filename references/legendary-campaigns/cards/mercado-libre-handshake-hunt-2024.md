# Handshake Hunt (Mercado Libre · 2024)

## Insight
People hate TV ads during Black Friday. They don't hate the content between the ads. Mercado Libre's logo is a handshake — the universal symbol of a good deal. Handshakes happen constantly in scripted television. If you place the deal inside the content rather than in the break between content, you have infiltrated the one place the audience didn't expect advertising to be.

## Mechanic
Instead of running conventional Black Friday ad breaks, Mercado Libre analyzed the entire script library of Globo (Brazil's second-largest TV network worldwide) to map every handshake moment in every show in advance. For each handshake that appeared on screen, a deal appeared for the category matching that content — musical instruments when a handshake occurred on a music show, TVs when it appeared in a movie, and so on. In one week, 50 million vouchers were distributed to 80 million people. When Black Friday ended, Mercado Libre then revealed the hunt was continuing — expanding beyond Globo to handshakes in content everywhere. The result was their best Black Friday in history by doing fewer ads.

## Why it worked
P01 (Format as Idea): the handshake is simultaneously a logo, a narrative event in scripted TV, and a cultural metaphor for deals — the concept only works because all three meanings align. P08 (Cultural Hijack): mapping an existing media property's content calendar in advance and injecting deals into moments the audience was already watching is a new form of cultural parasitism, benign and welcomed because the deal is relevant to what was just on screen. The category matching (handshake in a music show = music instrument discount) made the logic feel earned rather than opportunistic.

## Steal
When your logo or brand symbol has a real-world equivalent that appears in existing content, you can turn media placement into a game without owning the IP. Map where your symbol already occurs in culture. Then show up there instead of in the interruption.
