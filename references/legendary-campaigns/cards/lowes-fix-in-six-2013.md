# Fix in Six (Lowe's · 2013)

## Insight
Home improvement feels overwhelming and expensive. Six seconds of genuinely useful information — one clever trick, one real fix — makes it feel achievable. The constraint is the gift.

## Mechanic
Lowe's became one of the first brands to use Vine's 6-second format intentionally as the idea, not just as a channel. Each clip was a complete home improvement tip — stop-motion animation, clear problem/solution structure, zero wasted seconds. The format limitation produced the content quality.

## Why it worked
Most brands on new platforms try to adapt existing content. Lowe's recognised that Vine's six-second constraint was a creative brief, not a restriction. The format forced reduction to pure utility — which is the only thing a home improvement brand can credibly offer in six seconds.

## Steal
When using a constrained format (Vine, Twitter, TikTok 15-second), treat the constraint as the creative brief, not a problem to work around. The best content in constrained formats is designed for the constraint, not despite it.
