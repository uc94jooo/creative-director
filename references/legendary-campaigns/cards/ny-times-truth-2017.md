# The Truth Is Worth It (The New York Times · 2017)

## Insight
In an era of "fake news" and institutional distrust, The New York Times' greatest asset was not its brand name but the specific, verifiable, costly labor that goes into a single piece of investigative journalism. The gap between one true sentence and the investigation behind it is enormous.

## Mechanic
A series of films showing — in painstaking, real-time detail — the cost and process of getting a single fact right: the sources, the verification layers, the legal reviews, the courage required. Tagline: "The truth is worth it." Culminated in a Super Bowl film that listed the steps behind a single ISIS investigation.

## Why it worked
Long-form drama (P16): only extended runtime can show the full weight of what journalism costs. Truth telling (P13): the films do not argue that the Times is trustworthy — they demonstrate the process that makes trustworthiness. In the post-truth moment, process-as-proof was the most credible form of brand defence available.

## Steal
When your brand is under attack for what it does, show how it does it — in granular, time-consuming, unglamorous detail. The process is more credible than the claim. Showing work beats claiming excellence.
