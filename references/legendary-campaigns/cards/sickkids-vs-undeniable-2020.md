# VS: Undeniable (SickKids Foundation · 2020)

## Insight

Children in hospital are not victims — they are fighters. Charity advertising has trained audiences to feel pity, which creates emotional distance rather than engagement. These kids don't want pity; they want victory.

## Mechanic

Film presenting critically ill children at SickKids Hospital as warriors in a battle — visceral, sports-documentary-style filmmaking, pump-up music, warrior imagery — completely inverting the "sad sick children" charity aesthetic with defiant celebration of their strength.

## Why it worked

Long-form drama meets truth telling: the VS (versus) brand positioning framed disease as the enemy and the child as the combatant — not the victim. The emotional register shifted from tearful sympathy to rallying, energizing defiance. Donors fund warriors, not casualties.

## Steal

Find the most established emotional convention in your category. Ask whether it actually serves the audience it claims to. If not, invert the emotional register entirely. The contrast with category convention is the differentiation.
