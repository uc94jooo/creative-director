# Child Replacement Programme (Pedigree · 2016)

## Insight
When children leave home, many parents experience a gap in caregiving that they are emotionally unprepared for. Pedigree noticed that dog adoption rates spike significantly when children leave — a correlation that pointed to a real human need that no brand had addressed directly.

## Mechanic
An integrated campaign that openly marketed Pedigree dog adoption to "empty nesters" — parents whose children had recently left home — with the absurdist framing of literally replacing the child with a dog. Materials were crafted in the genre of family-planning communications.

## Why it worked
Behaviour inversion (P03): dog food brands talk about dogs; this campaign talked about a human emotional transition, with the dog as the solution. Absurdism (P11): the framing as a formal "programme" with clinical-style materials made the joke land while making the genuine insight feel sharper.

## Steal
Find the unspoken emotional context behind a purchase decision — the human situation the product actually resolves — and market to that situation rather than the product category. The category code becomes a supporting character.
