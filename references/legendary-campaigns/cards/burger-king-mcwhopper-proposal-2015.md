# The McWhopper Proposal (Burger King · 2015)

## Insight
Burger King and McDonald's are the most famous rivals in the world — but World Peace Day creates a cultural moment where a truce would be more newsworthy than any conventional ad.

## Mechanic
Burger King published an open letter to McDonald's CEO proposing a one-day truce: open a joint pop-up on World Peace Day, sell a hybrid burger (the McWhopper) and donate proceeds to Peace One Day. McDonald's declined publicly. BK won either way.

## Why it worked
McDonald's had no good response: accepting meant sharing the PR spotlight; declining made them the villain on World Peace Day. Burger King designed a public invitation where the competitor's only options both benefitted the challenger.

## Steal
Design a public invitation where the larger competitor has no favourable response. The chess move is the campaign — the competitor's reaction is the media.
