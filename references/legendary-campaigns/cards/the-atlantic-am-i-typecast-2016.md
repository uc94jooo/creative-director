# Am I Typecast? (The Atlantic · 2016)

## Insight
The Atlantic is perceived as the magazine for elite coastal intellectuals — a perception that limits its audience and undermines its journalism's reach. Naming that stereotype with ironic self-awareness is more disarming than denying it.

## Mechanic
A comedic film in which The Atlantic actors/voices playfully interrogate whether the publication (and by extension its readers) is "typecast" — smart, ironic, and self-aware enough to mock the very label that might repel new readers while charming existing ones.

## Why it worked
P11 (absurd as carrier) through self-directed comedy; P13 (truth-telling) by naming the cultural positioning that the brand typically avoids addressing. Publications rarely acknowledge their own stereotypes — doing so signals a confidence that actually attracts the audience it risks repelling.

## Steal
If your brand carries a specific cultural stereotype — elitist, niche, too serious — address it before your audience does. Self-aware comedy is more persuasive than earnest repositioning because it proves you already understand the criticism.
