# Tag (Nike · 2001)

## Mechanic

A TV film: a game of tag breaks out among adults in a city — business people, commuters, pedestrians — suddenly transformed into the completely absorbed seriousness of children playing. The game escalates through an entire urban landscape. The production scale (hundreds of participants, multiple city blocks) is matched by the sincerity of the participants' commitment to the game.

## Insight

The desire to play never leaves us — adults suppress it, but the moment permission exists, it erupts with full childhood intensity. Nike's "Just Do It" platform has always been about releasing the athlete (or child) inside the adult — Tag is the most literal, joyful expression of that idea.

## Why it worked

P11 (absurd as carrier): adults playing tag with absolute seriousness is inherently comic, but also genuinely moving — the viewer simultaneously laughs and feels the longing the film describes. P16 (long-form drama) gave the game time to escalate from one person to a citywide phenomenon, making the emotional payoff proportionate to the scale. The film requires no product message — the feeling it creates is the brand message.

## Steal

If a brand's emotional territory is about releasing joy or ability that's been suppressed, show the release rather than describing what's been suppressed. Find the simplest, most universal game and play it straight.
