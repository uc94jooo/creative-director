# Smarter Cities (IBM · 2013)

## Insight
IBM builds technology infrastructure for cities. Abstract corporate claims about "smarter cities" mean nothing to people experiencing a hot day, a rainstorm, or tired legs. What if the city's infrastructure itself became IBM's advertising medium?

## Mechanic
Ogilvy France created OOH installations that were also functional urban infrastructure: a billboard that became a rain shelter with a bench, a poster that directed pedestrians through a ramp, a billboard that formed a seating ledge. Each incorporated the Smarter Planet logo. The design IS the proof.

## Why it worked
P17 (Design as Idea): the outdoor unit is not a picture of a smarter city — it IS a smarter city intervention, in miniature. The gap between "advertising that claims" and "advertising that does" is eliminated. IBM's competency is demonstrated in the format of the ad.

## Steal
When your brand's promise is about improving public infrastructure or human experience, make your advertising do exactly that — at whatever scale your budget allows. The smallest functional improvement outperforms the largest claim.
