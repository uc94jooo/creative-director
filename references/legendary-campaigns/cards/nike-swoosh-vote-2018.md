# Swoosh Vote (Nike · 2018)

## Insight
Young Black Americans feel their civic power is routinely diminished — but sport has always been a space where they set the terms. Nike owns that space; channelling it into voter engagement is the only non-hypocritical call to action the brand can make.

## Mechanic
Nike turned the Swoosh into a voting symbol during the 2018 US midterms — integrated campaign with athlete-led content, social amplification, and a direct voter-registration mechanic. Users could interact and share pledges tied to the brand's Equality platform.

## Why it worked
The Swoosh is already a cultural badge of identity for the demographic Nike was reaching. Attaching voter registration to that badge made the ask feel native, not corporate. P09 (user as co-author) scaled reach through personal sharing; P04 (brand as activist) gave it credibility by linking to Nike's existing Equality work, not arriving from nowhere.

## Steal
If your brand has a pre-existing cultural symbol with genuine meaning for an audience, you can load that symbol with new civic weight without explaining yourself. The symbol does the persuading; you only need to point.
