# Hands (Honda · 2014)

## Insight
Honda's history is not a corporate timeline — it is a story of human hands making things. Every innovation the company ever produced began with someone building something with their hands. The brand's soul lives in that act of making, not in the product that results.

## Mechanic
A two-minute animated film that traces Honda's entire history — from Soichiro Honda's motorbikes to Formula 1 engines to humanoid robots — through a single pair of hands that are constantly building, tinkering, and creating. The hands grow and age. The technologies they hold become more complex. The throughline is always the same: making.

## Why it worked
P16 (long-form drama): a genuine emotional arc — the growth and aging of the hands maps onto the brand's generational ambition. P08 (craft as message): the animation itself is extraordinarily crafted, with hundreds of hand-drawn frames achieving a stop-motion quality that demonstrates the obsessive making-culture it describes. The brand celebrates its own origin story through the most direct means possible: showing hands that build things.

## Steal
Find the irreducible human gesture at the core of your brand's origin. Not the product, not the mission statement — the physical act that started everything. Tell the brand's entire history through that single gesture.
