# Never Say No to Panda (Panda Cheese · 2010)

## Insight
Mild peer pressure around food choices is relatable — someone offers you something; you decline politely; life continues. The campaign inverts the social contract entirely: what if refusing food had absurdly disproportionate consequences administered by a silent, inexorable panda?

## Mechanic
A series of spots in which a person declines Panda Cheese in a mundane setting. A giant panda appears, stares at the person, and then destroys something in the immediate environment — smashing a display case, overturning a hospital bed, ruining an office — while maintaining complete silence and an expression of pure neutrality. No explanation. No resolution. Tagline: "Never say no to Panda." Egyptian-produced, became a global viral hit.

## Why it worked
P11 (absurd as carrier): the absurdism works through its commitment to silence and impassivity — the panda never reacts, never explains. The humor comes entirely from the gap between the mundane trigger (declining a snack) and the catastrophic response. Made on a tiny budget by an Egyptian brand and became internationally known, demonstrating that absurdist humor is genuinely culture-neutral when committed to completely.

## Steal
Build a character whose internal logic is consistent even when it defies all external logic. The panda's power comes from its refusal to acknowledge that anything is wrong. The moment the panda explains itself, the joke is dead.
