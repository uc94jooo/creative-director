# Impossible Dream (Honda · 2005)

## Insight
Honda's founder Soichiro Honda believed that any dream is achievable if you work hard enough and persist long enough. The company's entire product history — from motorcycles to aircraft — is the evidence. The dream is not a slogan; it is the actual operating principle of the company.

## Mechanic
A two-minute epic film following a man on an extraordinary journey — climbing mountains, sailing seas, flying planes, riding rocket sleds — set to Matt Monro's "The Impossible Dream." Each vehicle he uses in the journey is a Honda product. The film ends on a jet that vanishes into the sky. No product close-ups. No claims. Only the journey.

## Why it worked
P16 (long-form drama): the emotional arc maps onto Honda's brand arc — perseverance, adventure, and the pursuit of impossible goals. The Matt Monro song does the brand's work at the emotional level, leaving the visuals to demonstrate product breadth without advertising it. The brand disappears into the journey and emerges more present for it.

## Steal
When your brand has a genuine origin story rooted in relentless pursuit, let the journey structure the film. Every product becomes an episode in the journey rather than a product shot. The result is a brand film that audiences watch willingly.
