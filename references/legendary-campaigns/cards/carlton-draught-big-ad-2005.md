# Big Ad (Carlton Draught · 2005)

## Insight
Beer advertising in Australia had settled into two modes: serious "mates" narratives and lightweight comedy. Neither was self-aware about the conventions of beer advertising itself. The category had accumulated so many conventions — big music, epic visuals, slow motion — that the conventions themselves were ripe for satire.

## Mechanic
An epic, sweeping cinematic commercial — hundreds of men running across a hillside, orchestral music swelling — with a self-referential narration: "This is a big ad. A big, big ad." The ad knowingly advertises the fact that it is a large, expensive advertisement for beer. The final tagline acknowledges the cost: "Be worth it." The joke is the meta-awareness of the ad about itself.

## Why it worked
P11 (absurdism): the absurdism is meta — the commercial's self-awareness of its own conventions is the joke. P03 (behavior inversion): beer advertising conventions (epic scale, slow motion, masculine scenery) are deployed and simultaneously mocked. The Australian deadpan sensibility made the self-reference feel genuinely funny rather than arch or clever. The "Big Ad" spread rapidly in the early YouTube era — before pre-roll advertising existed, audiences shared ads they found genuinely amusing. Cannes recognition validated international legibility of the Australian humor register.

## Steal
Every established advertising category has conventions so embedded they are invisible. Name them. Deploy them sincerely and simultaneously mock them. The meta-awareness of the ad about its own genre is only available to brands confident enough in their identity to not need the conventions to do the work.
