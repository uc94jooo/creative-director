# Icons (The Sunday Times · 2014)

## Insight
The Sunday Times is a newspaper with a century of history covering the people who shaped Britain and the world — but readers don't feel that legacy as a lived dimension of the paper.

## Mechanic
A film showing iconic figures from history in vivid colour, presented as if photographed today — connecting the contemporary reader to the paper's historical witness. Visual style makes the past feel present.

## Why it worked
The craft of the film elevated the brand's historical role. By presenting history with cinematic presence, The Sunday Times asserted that journalism shapes how posterity sees icons — and that reading the paper places the reader in that tradition.

## Steal
Use the visual language of contemporary prestige photography to present archival or historical material. Removing temporal distance through craft makes institutional heritage feel alive rather than dusty.
