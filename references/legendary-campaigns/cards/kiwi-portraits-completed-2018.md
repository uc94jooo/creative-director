# Portraits Completed (Kiwi · 2018)

## Insight
Iconic portraits throughout history show subjects from head to toe — but the shoes are often cut off by the frame. The one element that Kiwi polishes is systematically excluded from the most famous images in art history.

## Mechanic
Kiwi extended famous historical portraits — Mona Lisa, American Gothic, Girl with a Pearl Earring — below the original frame to reveal the subjects' shoes, polished to perfection. The extensions were executed in the style of the originals by skilled illustrators.

## Why it worked
The design insight is the entire idea: the product exists in the missing part of the frame. No copy needed — the implication (that great people wore great shoes, now revealed) communicates the brand's role without stating it. The executions generate curiosity about what is below any frame.

## Steal
Find the famous images in your world that systematically exclude your product's territory. Extend them into that territory. Let the implication do the work.
