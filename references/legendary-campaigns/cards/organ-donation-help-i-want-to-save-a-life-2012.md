# Help I Want to Save a Life (Organ Donation · 2012)

## Insight
The barrier to organ donation registration is not opposition — it's inertia. Most people who haven't registered simply haven't been given a moment that made it feel urgent and easy simultaneously.

## Mechanic
A social campaign turning everyday platforms into organ donor sign-up moments. Users who declared their intention to register were given shareable content and tools to encourage their network to do the same, creating a chain of commitment rather than a broadcast message.

## Why it worked
Peer-to-peer commitment is more effective than institutional messaging for behavior change. The social layer transformed passive awareness into active social contracts between friends.

## Steal
For behaviour change campaigns, design the commitment moment first — the smallest possible action that creates a social contract. Then build the awareness campaign around that moment, not the other way around.
