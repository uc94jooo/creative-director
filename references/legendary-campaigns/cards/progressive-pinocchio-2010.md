# Pinocchio (Progressive · 2010)

## Insight
Insurance customers suspect they're being lied to constantly. The absurdist move: what if the spokesperson's nose actually grew with every misleading claim, making the industry's dishonesty physically visible?

## Mechanic
A film playing on the Pinocchio fairy-tale trope applied to insurance industry claims — visible, literal lying made visual and absurd, with the honest alternative offered as contrast.

## Why it worked
Using a universally understood symbol of lying (the growing nose) to satirise the insurance industry's communication habits positioned Progressive as the honest alternative without making a direct competitive attack. The absurdity was the credibility mechanism.

## Steal
Category conventions that involve implicit dishonesty (price anchoring, hidden fees, fine print) can be exposed through fairy-tale or folk-tale archetypes without legal risk. Pinocchio is recognisable shorthand that doesn't require naming a competitor.
