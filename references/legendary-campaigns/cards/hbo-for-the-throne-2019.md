# For the Throne (HBO · 2019)

## Insight
Game of Thrones had created a global community unified by a single narrative tension: who would take the Iron Throne? Before the final season, that anticipation was the most valuable cultural moment HBO had ever owned. The campaign's job was not to build awareness — it was to channel existing obsession.

## Mechanic
An integrated campaign that invited fans to pledge allegiance to their chosen claimant — Daenerys, Jon, Cersei, etc. — through physical experiences, branded collaborations (Bud Light, Mountain Dew, Oreo), social content, and real-world events. The "For the Throne" framing made every consumer action a declaration of loyalty.

## Why it worked
User as co-author (P09): every pledge, share, and debate made fans active participants in the pre-season narrative. Serialization and ritual (P10): the show had built multi-year rituals (watch parties, recap culture, theory communities) that the campaign extended into physical form. The collaborations were genuine — Bud Light made a GoT Super Bowl spot; Oreo recreated the opening sequence in cookies.

## Steal
When you have a property with a built-in, fanatical audience, the campaign is to give that audience something to do — not something to watch. Convert passive viewership into active allegiance through real-world mechanics that cost nothing to participate in.
