# Viva la Vulva (Essity / Libresse · 2018)

## Insight
Women have no visual reference for what a "normal" vulva looks like — the only representation in mainstream culture is pornographic, creating widespread body anxiety about something every woman has. The anatomy had been systematically excluded from visual culture.

## Mechanic
A musical film and integrated campaign featuring vulvas represented through everyday objects — shells, fruits, flowers, sculptures — all singing a celebratory song. Joyful, aesthetically rich, anatomically specific without being clinical. OOH and social extensions.

## Why it worked
Behaviour inversion (P03): fashion and beauty advertising had eliminated the vulva from representation; Libresse built an entire campaign around it. Design as idea (P17): the objects chosen as visual metaphors were carefully selected to be beautiful, not shocking — making the campaign accessible to the broadest possible audience. The humour was the Trojan horse that delivered a message that drama would have made defensive.

## Steal
Serious body-image or health topics are often better approached through aesthetic delight and gentle humour than through earnest drama. Beauty lowers defences; seriousness raises them. The audience leans in when the thing is beautiful.
