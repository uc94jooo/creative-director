# Body Bags (Truth · 1998)

## Insight
Tobacco companies knew their product killed people and hid it. Young people were angry, but had no visceral, undeniable proof delivered at scale. Telling teens "smoking kills" changed nothing — showing who killed whom could.

## Mechanic
Youth activists delivered 1,200 body bags to the headquarters of Philip Morris in New York City — one for each person who dies daily from tobacco-related illness in the United States. The stunt was filmed, amplified through TV spots, and became the launch of the Truth anti-tobacco campaign that would define a generation.

## Why it worked
The body bags made an abstract statistic physical and unmovable. Philip Morris could not spin 1,200 bags on their doorstep. The action gave young people agency — they were the activists, not the victims. Truth inverted the power dynamic: instead of vulnerable teens needing protection, teens were confronting a corporation. P17 element: the visual language of the bags was design-as-message — no headline needed.

## Steal
When a statistic has been cited so often it no longer lands — make it physical and unmovable. Body count = body bags. Abstract death toll = concrete objects delivered to the responsible party. The action forces a confrontation the villain cannot walk away from.
