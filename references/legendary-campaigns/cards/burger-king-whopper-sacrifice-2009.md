# Whopper Sacrifice (Burger King · 2009)

## Insight
Facebook had just introduced the concept of accumulating "friends" as social currency. Everyone claimed their friends list mattered. But did it really? BK identified a cultural tension: people call hundreds of acquaintances "friends" but the word has been devalued. What is a Whopper worth compared to a Facebook friendship?

## Mechanic
A Facebook app: delete 10 Facebook friends, get a coupon for a free Whopper. The app notified the deleted friends that they had been sacrificed for a burger. The social notification was the mechanic — the humiliation of being worth less than one-tenth of a Whopper was the joke. Over 230,000 friends were deleted before Facebook shut the app down for violating privacy policies. The shutdown generated more PR than the campaign itself.

## Why it worked
The idea exposed the hollow currency of Facebook friendship at the exact cultural moment when "friend" was being redefined by the platform. Getting unfriended because of a burger was funny, legible, and human. The app shutdown by Facebook was the second act — BK became the brand that Facebook was afraid of. The absurdism (P11) was structural: the idea that a friendship has a monetary value (1/10 of a Whopper) and can be compared publicly.

## Steal
Find where social norms have created inflated currency (followers, friends, likes) and expose the inflation with a mechanic that puts a price on it. The social discomfort of the exposure is the campaign — not the coupon. Being shut down by a platform is free PR worth more than media spend.
