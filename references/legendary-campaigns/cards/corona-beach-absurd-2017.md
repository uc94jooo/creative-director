# Beach (Corona · 2017)

## Insight
Beer brand communication lives in one of two registers — aspirational lifestyle fantasy or self-aware irony. Corona's beach mythology is so established it can be gently mocked from within, which is more charming than either earnest execution.

## Mechanic
A comedic film using Corona's own beach-and-sunshine iconography as the raw material for absurdist scenarios — taking the brand's established visual language to its logical extreme with a comedic twist that acknowledges the artifice.

## Why it worked
P11 (absurd as carrier) applied to a brand that already owns a strong visual territory — the absurdism works because the baseline is well-established. Self-referential humour from a brand with a clear visual identity is more credible than the same approach from a brand without one.

## Steal
When your brand has a visual territory so established it is almost a cliché, the next creative move is to inhabit that cliché with full awareness. The brand that knows it owns a trope can play with the trope — the brand that doesn't know it can't.
