# Noah (Miller Genuine Draft · 2000)

## Insight
Beer advertising in the late 1990s was dominated by laddish humor and simplistic lifestyle imagery. Miller Genuine Draft commissioned director Errol Morris to apply his documentary portrait style to everyday American characters, creating something warmer and stranger than the category norm.

## Mechanic
A short character study — part of Errol Morris's Miller Genuine Draft campaign — featuring a real or real-feeling American persona in an intimate, slightly absurd conversation format. The beer functions as ambient context rather than hero product.

## Why it worked
Morris's documentary aesthetic imported the credibility of non-fiction filmmaking into beer advertising. The characters feel found rather than cast. The result is genuinely human and unexpected in a category built on archetypes.

## Steal
When the category uses fictional archetypes, recruit documentary aesthetics. Real people behave in ways actors can't predict — and audiences read the difference. The brand benefit is transferred through the authenticity of the format, not through a claim.
