# Turning Their Tweets Against Them (Burger King · 2017)

## Mechanic
Burger King promoted tweets in which customers complained about rivals — specifically Wendy's fans demanding the return of spicy nuggets — essentially using competitor-brand conversation as free media against that competitor.

## Insight
Social media surfaces competitor dissatisfaction in public. Any brand with the nerve to amplify that dissatisfaction gains both reach and credibility — the complaint comes from real users, not the advertiser.

## Why it worked
Enemy or conflict (P02): the adversarial positioning is explicit but the weapon is the competitor's own unhappy customers. User-as-co-author (P09): the complainants did the creative work; BK just amplified and contextualised it. Zero production cost, maximum provocation.

## Steal
Monitor social for authentic competitor dissatisfaction. Promoting real customer complaints against a rival costs almost nothing and reads as more credible than any attack ad. The competitor's weakness becomes your ad budget.
