# Errol Morris Series (Miller Genuine Draft · 2000)

## Insight
Beer advertising operated with a standardized vocabulary of aspiration: sports, parties, attractive people, simple humor. Miller Genuine Draft recognized that an entire register of American life — the eccentric, the specific, the quietly odd — had been completely ignored.

## Mechanic
A multi-spot campaign commissioning documentary filmmaker Errol Morris to create a series of character portraits. Real and real-feeling Americans spoke directly to camera about their opinions, habits, and obsessions — with MGD present as ambient atmosphere rather than product hero.

## Why it worked
The campaign functions as a single advertising idea applied across many executions: "authentic American character." Morris's visual grammar (Interrotron technique, direct address) imported documentary credibility into the paid media environment. Each spot is distinct but the series builds cumulative brand personality.

## Steal
When commissioning a campaign, hire a director whose existing body of work already embodies your brand's values. The filmmaking style is part of the brief. Don't hire a documentary filmmaker and then brief them to make a commercial.
