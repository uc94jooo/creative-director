# Phones Are Good (Three UK · 2018)

## Insight
Every telecom brand communicates as if smartphones are tools for genuine human connection. The cultural discourse, meanwhile, treats smartphones as a source of addiction, distraction, and disconnection. Three UK took the side the industry never takes: phones are, actually, good.

## Mechanic
A series of absurdist vignettes celebrating the genuinely useful and pleasurable things people do with phones — delivered with the straight-faced absurdist tone that Wieden+Kennedy London had developed for the brand. Counter-intuitive because it says something the entire industry knows but everyone is too earnest to state.

## Why it worked
P11 (absurd as carrier) combined with a genuinely contrarian brand position: in a cultural moment of phone-shaming, Three UK said "no, phones are great" and meant it. The campaign's confidence is the differentiator — most telecom brands hedge their message with responsibility disclaimers. Three owned the pro-phone position completely.

## Steal
When your entire category is walking away from its core product benefit under cultural pressure, own the benefit more aggressively than ever. The brand that doesn't apologise for what it sells stands out in a category of apologetic competitors.
