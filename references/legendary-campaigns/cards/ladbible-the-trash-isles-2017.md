# The Trash Isles (LadBible · 2017)

## Insight
The Great Pacific Garbage Patch is larger than France, but because it belongs to no nation, no international body is responsible for it — and no legal mechanism exists to force action. The absence of sovereignty was the absence of accountability.

## Mechanic
LadBible submitted an official application to the United Nations to recognise the Pacific Garbage Patch as a sovereign nation — The Trash Isles — complete with a flag (plastic bags), passport, currency (debris), stamps, and a national anthem. Al Gore became Citizen #1. Over 100,000 people signed for citizenship. The application was formally submitted to the UN.

## Why it worked
By giving an amorph problem the institutional form of a nation-state, the campaign created a story with a subject, a flag, and a legal hook that every news outlet could report on. Brand as activist (P04): a real institutional action at genuine cost. User as co-author (P09): each citizenship registration was a piece of content and a data point for the petition.

## Steal
Turn an invisible, amorph problem into a formal institutional object — a country, a corporation, a legal case. This creates a subject that press and public can interact with. The legal absurdity amplifies, not undermines, the seriousness of the underlying issue.
