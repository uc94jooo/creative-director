# Inglorious Fruits and Vegetables (Intermarché · 2014)

## Insight
30% of produce is discarded before reaching stores because it fails cosmetic standards. Consumers have been trained to reject misshapen food on appearance alone, not quality. Nobody challenged that conditioning because it was invisible — the ugly produce was never shown.

## Mechanic
Intermarché created a dedicated in-store section selling visually imperfect fruits and vegetables at 30% discount. Each variety had a campaign name ("The Ugly Carrot," "The Ridiculous Potato"), packaging with personality, and recipes demonstrating that ugly produces identical food to beautiful. Supported by TV, print, and tasting events.

## Why it worked
The inversion target was maximally precise: the cosmetic standard, not the food industry broadly. By creating a dedicated display with its own branding, Intermarché made "ugly produce" desirable rather than charity. The 30% discount gave rational permission to the emotional reframing. Sales of the non-ugly produce also increased — shoppers reconsidered the whole category.

## Steal
When an industry convention causes a measurable social harm, make that harm visible and offer an alternative in the same breath. Don't campaign against the convention — design around it. Product-as-solution, not communications-as-criticism.
