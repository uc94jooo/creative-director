# The March of the Emperors (Canal+ · 2006)

## Insight
The nature documentary format carries a peculiar cultural authority — it makes anything filmed in its style feel important and worth watching. Canal+ subscribers, as cinema lovers, would appreciate a brand that uses the format's authority as a joke rather than a crutch.

## Mechanic
A spot parodying the style of "March of the Penguins" (the 2005 documentary hit). Penguins march in the familiar fashion, narrated with documentary gravitas — until they arrive at a cinema and queue for tickets. The joke is earned because the format is respected, not mocked. Canal+'s tagline: "Committed to Cinema."

## Why it worked
P11 (absurd as carrier): the cultural context of the original film makes the parody work — audiences who saw "March of the Penguins" experience the delight of recognition. P16 (long-form drama): the documentary format has its own emotional logic, which the spot borrows and then playfully undermines. BETC's Canal+ work established the template that "The Bear" would perfect five years later.

## Steal
Parody works only when you respect the original. The penguins going to the cinema is funny because the preceding shots are genuinely good nature-documentary filmmaking. A cheap parody would have been a cheaper joke.
