# Hungerithm (Snickers · 2016)

## Insight
"You're not you when you're hungry" is Snickers' global advertising idea — but it had never been proven in real time. Collective online anger is a measurable proxy for collective hunger; if the internet is furious, people need a Snickers.

## Mechanic
An algorithm monitored Twitter sentiment in Australia in real time — tracking the collective anger level of the internet. The angrier the internet became, the cheaper Snickers got at 7-Eleven: the price dropped up to 82% at peak rage, updating 144 times per day. Users could check the current price at any time.

## Why it worked
Technology as canvas (P18): the algorithm was not a distribution gimmick but a literal instantiation of the brand's advertising idea — it converted the slogan into a working mechanism. User as co-author (P09): every angry tweet became part of the pricing mechanism without requiring direct participation. The idea proved itself by working.

## Steal
Take your brand's core slogan and design a mechanism that executes it in the real world rather than communicates it in media. "Show, don't tell" applied to brand platforms: the idea becomes a product, not a message.
