# Spreadbeats (Spotify · 2024)

## Insight
Spotify's pitch deck to advertising clients is a spreadsheet — a medium associated with rational data and commercial transaction. The irony is that Spotify is selling emotional experiences: music, connection, memory. If the media plan itself could become a music video, the gap between the spreadsheet's coldness and music's warmth would become the creative statement: emotion can be found anywhere, even in the most rational format.

## Mechanic
Spotify coded an entire music video into a single 10-megabyte media plan spreadsheet file and delivered it to advertising clients as their B2B pitch material. The video used no external libraries — every visual element was built from native spreadsheet functions: Unicode characters from obscure alphabets became imagery, ASCII values were repurposed as visualization tools, conditional formatting governed visual color universes, quad-tree algorithms from the 1970s handled image compression, and curve-based animation from the same era handled motion. The music track was stored in a separate tab and synced to visuals within a 5–15 millisecond range imperceptible to human senses. The protagonist was E7 — a cell in column E, row 7 — who journeys from rational data toward emotional complexity. The entire production was digital archaeology: techniques unused for 30 years, repurposed inside a commercial format.

## Why it worked
P01 (Format as Idea): the spreadsheet is simultaneously the delivery mechanism, the creative canvas, and the conceptual argument — you cannot separate the format from the message. P12 (Data Made Human): the most data-rational format in existence was used to express the human need for beauty and emotion. E7's narrative — a data point that longs to become more than a number — is a metaphor for the entire Spotify pitch: data should make you feel something. The use of 1970s compression methods in a 2024 campaign paradoxically demonstrated more technical sophistication than contemporary tools would have allowed.

## Steal
When pitching to a rational, data-oriented buyer, find the format they use to process information and make that format do the emotional work instead. The medium's unexpectedness amplifies the message's impact. If you can make a spreadsheet emotional, you've already made your argument about what your platform does.
