# If You're Into It, It's in the V&A (Victoria & Albert Museum · 2024)

## Insight
The V&A holds one of the world's most diverse collections — fashion, gaming, jewellery, theatre, technology — but is perceived as a highbrow institution for a narrow cultural elite.

## Mechanic
A poster campaign using the typographic format of internet subculture — bold, democratic, direct — connecting specific niche interests ("If you're into sneakers…", "If you're into K-pop…") directly to V&A collections. Design language borrowed from street culture, not heritage institutions.

## Why it worked
The message (everything is here) was delivered through the visual language of the people who thought it wasn't for them. The design inversion was the argument: the institution that looks like this cannot be stuffy.

## Steal
Adopt the visual vernacular of your most alienated potential audience to communicate the message that the institution contains their world. Design style is the proof; copy is the invitation.
