# It's a Tide Ad (Tide · 2018)

## Insight

Every Super Bowl ad featuring clean clothes, people looking good, or domestic happiness is implicitly an ad for Tide — even if it's advertising a car, beer, or perfume. Tide owns the visual of clean.

## Mechanic

Series of Super Bowl spots that begin looking like ads for other categories (beer, luxury cars, cologne) before David Harbour from Stranger Things appears and announces "This is a Tide Ad." Every Super Bowl ad with clean clothes becomes suspect. Cultural hijack of the entire advertising break.

## Why it worked

Format as idea meets cultural hijack meets absurdism: the campaign used the meta-awareness of the Super Bowl advertising environment as the creative territory. By claiming all clean-clothes imagery as Tide's domain, they occupied the entire break — not just their own spots. The format (appearing in other ads) was the idea.

## Steal

Find the category property your brand legitimately owns that nobody has explicitly claimed. Build a campaign that claims it loudly. The claim itself is the disruption.
