# Flightless Birds (Washington Lottery · 2011)

## Insight
Lottery advertising typically promises fantasy wealth — but the truth is that most people play the lottery not for the jackpot but for the brief permission to dream. "Flightless Birds" may have acknowledged the absurdity of that dream while celebrating it.

## Mechanic
An absurdist film (P11) using flightless birds as a metaphor — likely for people who dream of flight (winning) but are grounded by reality. The truth-telling element (P13) acknowledges the lottery's reality while finding dignity in the dream.

## Why it worked
Lottery advertising that acknowledges its own absurdity while celebrating the dream is more honest than pure fantasy advertising. The flightless bird metaphor is specific and unexpected — birds that should fly but can't, people who want to soar but probably won't.

## Steal
When your product's primary value is the permission to dream rather than a likely outcome, sell the dream while acknowledging its improbability. The honesty makes the dream more lovable, not less.
