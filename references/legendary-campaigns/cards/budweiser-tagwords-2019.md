# TagWords (Budweiser · 2019)

## Insight
Social media users routinely tag alcohol brands they're drinking in their posts. These UGC tags are free testimonials. What if you printed those exact tags as OOH ads — making the social conversation into billboard copy?

## Mechanic
Print and OOH campaign using real Instagram hashtags and location tags that users had organically applied when drinking Budweiser — "#budweiser", venue names, events — displayed as the ad copy itself. The user-generated language became the headline, bypassing the need for a copywriter's claim.

## Why it worked
Design as idea (P17): the tags are the visual and the concept simultaneously. Behavior inversion (P03): instead of the brand talking about itself, the brand demonstrates that consumers already talk about it. The meta-quality — advertising that shows people already advertising for you — communicates brand love more powerfully than any claim of brand love.

## Steal
Mine your organic social tags for OOH copy. User-generated language in premium media placements conveys cultural relevance that no brief-driven headline can match. The contrast between the informal tag syntax and the formal billboard medium creates productive cognitive friction.
