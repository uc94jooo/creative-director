# Periodsomnia (Essity / Libresse · 2022)

## Insight
73% of women report that menstrual pain disrupts their sleep — one of the most widely shared physical experiences in the world, never shown in advertising. The category had been talking about periods in daylight for decades while the night-time reality remained invisible.

## Mechanic
A surrealist film depicting women's menstrual suffering at night — visualised through transformation, monsters, crawling, nightmare sequences. No euphemism, no blue liquid, no smiling woman in white trousers. The product appears as relief, not the hero.

## Why it worked
Libresse's consistent escalation strategy (Bloodnormal → Viva la Vulva → Womb Stories → Periodsomnia) means each new execution must break a new taboo. Having established that the brand shows the truth, audiences expect — and want — each iteration to go further. The behaviour inversion (P03) is from "sanitised category imagery" to "surrealist horror"; the truth-telling (P13) is naming a universal experience that the category had collectively avoided for a generation.

## Steal
Once a brand has broken one category taboo, the audience expects escalation. Retreating to convention after a breakthrough is the single most damaging creative decision a challenger brand can make.
