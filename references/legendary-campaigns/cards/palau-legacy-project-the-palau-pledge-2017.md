# The Palau Pledge (Palau Legacy Project · 2017)

## Insight

Palau's fragile ecosystem is damaged by tourism, but the government cannot directly tell tourists to behave — that kills the welcome and the income. But children asking adults to protect their home is a different conversation.

## Mechanic

The Palau government embedded a pledge into passport stamps on entry: an eco-promise written from the perspective of Palauan children, asking each visitor to sign it as a condition of entry. No signature, no visa. The government made conservation a legal/administrative requirement — expressed as a moral invitation.

## Why it worked

User as co-author meets brand as activist: the pledge was co-authored by children (their voices, their words) and signed by every tourist — making compliance feel like a gift to children rather than a government rule. Embedding communication in the border control process made it impossible to ignore.

## Steal

Find an obligatory institutional process your audience already goes through. Ask whether the communication in that process serves the most important message. If not, redesign the communication in that moment of capture.
