# Jordan 1, Newton 0 (Nike · 1993)

## Insight

Michael Jordan's hang time and vertical leap were so extraordinary they appeared to literally defy gravity. Isaac Newton's laws of physics were the highest possible authority to invoke — and Jordan had apparently beaten them.

## Mechanic

A print ad with a single line: "Jordan 1, Newton 0." An image of Jordan in the air. The Swoosh. Nothing else. The copy scores a sporting result against the foundational law of physics as if it were a rival team. The joke required no explanation — the audience immediately understood the scoreboard format and the magnitude of the claim.

## Why it worked

P17 (design as idea): the scoreboard format is the entire idea — presenting a scientific constant as a losing opponent. The wit is structural: sports language applied to physics. The economy of the execution (four words, one image) demonstrated creative confidence — no padding, no explanation, no softening. It stands as a masterclass in print economy.

## Steal

Find the highest possible authority figure in your category and position your brand or its hero as having surpassed them. The authority's credibility amplifies the claim rather than undermining it. Make the format do the work: a scoreboard implies competition, victory, and finality in the most compressed way possible.
