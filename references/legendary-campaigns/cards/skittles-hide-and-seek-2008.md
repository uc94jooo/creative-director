# Hide & Seek (Skittles · 2008)

## Insight
TODO: Verify exact year. Part of Skittles' "Touch the Rainbow" campaign — a sustained absurdist platform that ran from the mid-2000s through the 2010s.

## Mechanic
A Skittles spot using hide-and-seek as the premise for an absurdist scenario — likely involving the transformation of objects into Skittles or an unexpected twist on the childhood game delivered with deadpan sincerity.

## Why it worked
The campaign's power came from consistency: every execution used a different absurd setup but the same straight-faced commitment. The "hide and seek" framing provides a familiar structure that the brand then fills with completely illogical content.

## Steal
Use familiar social rituals (games, conversations, transactions) as scaffolding for absurd scenarios. The audience's familiarity with the ritual makes the departure from it funnier and sharper.
