# Hump Day (GEICO · 2013)

## Insight
Wednesday is "Hump Day" — the weekly nadir before the weekend relief. Insurance advertising is also a hump: a necessary, joyless purchase. GEICO's consistent strategy is to make the joyless joyful — and a camel loudly celebrating Wednesday is purely, inexplicably joyful.

## Mechanic
An office scene. A camel wanders through, announcing "HUMP DAYYY!" to indifferent employees. Pure non-sequitur. No direct product connection beyond the GEICO brand. The camel became a cultural meme, shared independently of any media buy.

## Why it worked
GEICO's brand permission — pure absurdism in service of making insurance less dreadful — was broad enough to make a wordplay-based camel film feel on-brand. The sharability came from the camel's pure, unexplained enthusiasm. People shared it because it made them happy, not because they engaged with GEICO.

## Steal
When a brand's territory is "relief from joylessness," pure joy with no product tether can work — but only if the brand already owns that tonal territory. GEICO had earned the right to make inexplicably funny ads. Without that equity, the camel is just a non-sequitur.
