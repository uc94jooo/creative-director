# Did You Mean Mailchimp? (Mailchimp · 2017)

## Insight
Mailchimp is a brand with an odd name that people often mishear or mispronounce. Rather than suppress this awkwardness or correct it, the campaign celebrates and amplifies it by creating 9 fictional brands with similar-sounding names.

## Mechanic
An integrated campaign built around 9 different creative executions — each for a fake brand with a phonetically similar name: MailShrimp, KaleLimp, JailBlimp, MaleCrimp, WailChimp, etc. Each fake brand was developed with full creative execution — film, site, merchandise — before revealing the actual brand. Design-as-idea (P17): the visual identity of each fake brand was fully realised.

## Why it worked
Absurd as carrier (P11): the premise is structurally funny — fully producing nine fake companies to advertise one real company. Design as idea (P17): each fake brand had a distinct and committed visual world. The campaign was more interesting to talk about than most campaigns, and talking about it was advertising. Earned press was enormous.

## Steal
Turn your brand's awkwardness into a campaign. The name people get wrong, the product people mispronounce, the image people misunderstand — these frictions are assets if you lean into them. The internet will talk about a brand that has the courage to laugh at itself.
