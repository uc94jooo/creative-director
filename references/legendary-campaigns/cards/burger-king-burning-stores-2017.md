# Burning Stores (Burger King · 2017)

## Insight
Burger King's Flame Grilling is the brand's defining product truth — and the most compelling proof of that truth is a restaurant actually on fire. Burger King has had multiple real locations catch fire over the years.

## Mechanic
Burger King ran print ads using real photographs of Burger King restaurants that had caught fire — framed as aspirational product photography. The burning building demonstrates the flame-grilling claim better than any stylised food shot. "Flame grilled since 1954."

## Why it worked
Behaviour inversion (P03): using disaster photography as brand advertising inverts every convention of QSR communication. Design as idea (P17): the image choice IS the argument — no copy required beyond the tagline. The audacity signals total brand confidence. The fires were real; the framing is the idea.

## Steal
Search your brand's archive for the moments that were disasters at the time. Then ask: does this disaster demonstrate the brand truth more honestly than any planned shoot? If yes, license it.
