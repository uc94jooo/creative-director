# The Talk (P&G · 2017)

## Insight
In America, Black mothers have a specific, decades-long tradition of preparing their children for encounters with the police — a conversation about how to behave so as not to be killed. Most white Americans don't know this conversation exists.

## Mechanic
A film showing Black mothers across multiple generations — 1960s, 1980s, 2000s, present — each having "The Talk" with their children: specific instructions about how to interact with police and authority in a way that keeps them safe. The conversations change in their wording but not in their necessity.

## Why it worked
P&G's brand platform is about "the best a person can be" across the family arc — a company with sufficient scale and cultural standing to address this without it being perceived as purely opportunistic. Social experiment (P12): the film doesn't editorialize or advocate; it shows a ritual so that viewers who have never heard of it can witness its reality. Long-form drama (P16): the generational structure required length to land the cumulative weight of the perpetuation across time.

## Steal
Universal themes like racism become specific and credible when reduced to a single, named, repeating ritual. "The Talk" is not about racism in general — it is about one specific conversation that proves the existence of a systemic problem without argument.
