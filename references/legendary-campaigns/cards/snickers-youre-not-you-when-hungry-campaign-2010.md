# You're Not You When You're Hungry (Snickers · 2010)

## Insight
Hunger doesn't just make you hungry — it makes you someone else. An inferior, embarrassing, wrong version of yourself. The solution is immediate and simple: a Snickers. The advertising idea maps directly to the product benefit without any mediation.

## Mechanic
An advertising platform built entirely on the displacement mechanic: person A is performing badly or acting out of character, revealed to be hungry, eats a Snickers, returns to being themselves. The variable is who plays person A — Betty White, Robin Williams, Willem Dafoe, a Diva — each casting creating a fresh execution of the same irreducible idea.

## Why it worked
The advertising idea (you become the wrong person when hungry) is infinitely generative: any celebrity miscast in any situation is a new Snickers ad. The idea doesn't exhaust — it compounds. Every new casting is funnier because the audience knows the formula and anticipates the reveal.

## Steal
An advertising idea that can run for 15+ years in 80+ markets must be simple enough to generate infinite variations and specific enough that each variation is immediately recognisable as itself. The Snickers formula: extreme mismatch + one Snickers + return to self. Three components; infinite casting.
