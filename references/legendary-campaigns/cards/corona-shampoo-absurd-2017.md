# Shampoo (Corona · 2017)

## Insight
Same as Beer/Beach series: Corona's beach aesthetic is strong enough to sustain absurdist parody that reinforces rather than undermines the brand's core sensation — escape, ease, the beach.

## Mechanic
Absurdist film placing Corona's sensory codes — sun, sea, relaxation — in a comedic incongruity with an everyday product category (shampoo), pushing brand imagery into surreal domestic territory.

## Why it worked
Part of the same absurdist series as "Beach," "Karate," and "ShottaSoCo." The series demonstrates that a brand with a fully-owned visual mythology can generate entertainment by displacing that mythology into unexpected contexts. Each film is a standalone execution of the same tension: Corona relaxation vs. mundane reality.

## Steal
A campaign series built from one absurdist engine can scale indefinitely — each entry is a new displacement of the same established mythology into a new incongruous context. The engine is the idea; each film is an execution.
