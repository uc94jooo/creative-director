# Salla 2032 (House of Lapland · 2021)

## Insight
Salla is one of the coldest towns in Finland — a place no one would consider for the Summer Olympics. But by 2032, climate change will have warmed it enough to host. The absurdity of the bid was the climate warning.

## Mechanic
The town of Salla submitted a genuine-looking Olympic bid for the Summer Games 2032, complete with an IOC-style presentation video, an official application, and athlete interviews delivered in deadpan seriousness. The implicit message: "If Salla can host the Summer Olympics, the climate has already lost." Earned global press.

## Why it worked
Brand as activist (P04): the municipality put its name and credibility behind a climate message, giving it institutional weight. User as co-author (P09): the bid format invited global media and audiences to participate by taking it seriously or sharing it. The joke and the argument are the same thing — you cannot explain the absurdity without explaining climate change.

## Steal
Use a serious institutional format (Olympic bid, UN petition, patent application) to deliver a serious argument through apparent absurdity. The format provides credibility; the gap between the form and the content is the idea. The press will explain the joke, and in doing so, spread the message.
