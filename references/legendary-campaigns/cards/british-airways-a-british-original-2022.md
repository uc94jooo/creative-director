# A British Original (British Airways · 2022)

## Insight
When an airline loses its premium status, the temptation is to sell destinations — but British Airways found that the real differentiator was the people flying: odd, stubborn, specific British individuals who couldn't exist anywhere else.

## Mechanic
A campaign of portrait films and OOH featuring real passengers and staff — their quirks, contradictions, and unapologetic Britishness — shot with restraint by Uncommon. Logo frequently cropped or minimal. Ran across cinema, OOH, and digital.

## Why it worked
In a category where every airline sells aspirational destinations equally, BA sold identity. The invisible-brand restraint (P07) signals confidence — only a truly established brand can afford to put a partial logo on 324 billboard sites. The specificity of British character gave international audiences a point of fascination rather than a generic premium promise.

## Steal
When your category is visually homogeneous, abandon category codes entirely and anchor in national or local characterology. Crop your own logo — only secure brands can do it, and the act itself communicates hierarchy.
