# Ketchup Fraud (Heinz · 2023)

## Insight
Restaurants routinely refill Heinz bottles with cheaper generic ketchup — a widespread deception that Heinz fans instinctively detect and resent, but which has never been named publicly.

## Mechanic
Heinz ran a campaign inviting consumers to post evidence of "ketchup fraud" — restaurants caught refilling Heinz bottles with other brands — turning customers into brand detectives and social media became the enforcement mechanism.

## Why it worked
The campaign weaponized existing consumer behaviour (noticing the fraud) by giving it a name, a platform, and a sense of righteous mission. The brand's existing fanbase did the work; Heinz supplied the frame.

## Steal
Find the behaviour your most loyal customers are already doing privately and give it a public name, a hashtag, and a platform. Naming the behaviour creates community; community creates content; content creates reach.
