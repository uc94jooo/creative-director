# K9FM (Pedigree · 2012)

## Insight
Shelter dogs spend most of their time alone in kennels, with no stimulation. Research suggested that music reduces stress in dogs. What if there was a radio station playing music specifically composed and selected for the auditory range and stress patterns of dogs?

## Mechanic
Pedigree launched K9FM — a 24/7 radio station broadcasting exclusively for dogs at shelters. Music was purpose-composed at frequencies and rhythms scientifically calibrated for canine anxiety. The absurdist premise (a radio station with no human audience) generated enormous PR; the underlying welfare intervention was genuinely real.

## Why it worked
Absurdism (P11): the premise is inherently ridiculous — a radio station nobody listens to — but the execution was rigorous and caring. The joke and the genuine welfare intervention operated simultaneously, which made the campaign feel warm rather than cynical.

## Steal
Absurdist premises that contain a sincere act of care are more powerful than purely comedic ones. The audience laughs and then feels something — the humour is the delivery mechanism for the sincerity, not a substitute for it.
