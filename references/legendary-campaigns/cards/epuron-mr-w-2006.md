# Mr. W (Epuron · 2006)

## Insight
Wind energy was abstract and invisible as a concept. Epuron, a German wind energy company, needed to make wind tangible and sympathetic. The insight: wind is everywhere, it affects everyone, but it is always the invisible disruptor — it messes hair, blows papers, knocks things over. What if wind had feelings about this?

## Mechanic
A 3-minute short film following a melancholic man (Mr. Wind) who tries to fit into human society but keeps accidentally causing chaos — blowing over displays, scattering papers, disrupting conversations. He falls in love. He cannot stop his nature. In the final scene he generates electricity for a lamp — for the first time, his power becomes useful. He finds his purpose.

## Why it worked
P11 absurdism: personifying wind as a depressed social outcast is surreal but internally logical — the film commits completely. P16 long-form drama: the 3-minute format allowed genuine character development and emotional investment in a non-human protagonist. The emotional payoff (wind finds purpose as energy) translated directly to the brand promise without any claim being made. Cannes-winning work for a company no consumer had ever heard of — the film spread because it was genuinely moving.

## Steal
Personify the invisible or the abstract. Give wind a face, give software a voice, give data a body. The personification must be internally consistent and emotionally true — the audience must care about the character before they care about the product.
