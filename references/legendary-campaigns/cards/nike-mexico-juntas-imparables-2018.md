# Juntas Imparables (Nike Mexico · 2018)

## Insight
In Mexico, girls who play sport are constantly told why they shouldn't: too aggressive, too unfeminine, too visible. The barriers are not physical — they are cultural permissions that women must negotiate every day before the game even starts.

## Mechanic
A film following Mexican female athletes — from footballer Sara Martínez to wrestler Yendira Segura — showing the specific cultural friction they face at home, in their communities, and in public before they reach their sport. Title: "Juntas Imparables" (Unstoppable Together). Narrated in Spanish for a local audience.

## Why it worked
Rather than importing the universal "Dream Crazy" message, Nike Mexico built a culturally specific articulation of the same insight — making the friction visible and named in a context where it is normally invisible. P13 (truth-telling) localised; P04 (brand as activist) grounded in Nike's commitment to women's sport investment in the market. The specificity was the strength: a Mexican girl watching saw her exact barriers, not a generic story of female empowerment.

## Steal
Global brand ideas need local truth, not local translation. Find the specific barrier in the specific culture — the one that is rarely named — and name it precisely. The cultural friction is the insight; the brand is the permission to overcome it.
