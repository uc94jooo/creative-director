# Chicken (Mercedes-Benz · 2013)

## Insight
Magic Body Control — a suspension system that keeps the car level on any terrain — is an invisible technology. The smoother the ride, the less you notice it. Making invisibility visible requires an analogy everyone can instantly understand.

## Mechanic
A viral film showing chickens walking in slow motion, their heads staying perfectly still while their bodies bob with each step — demonstrating the same stabilization principle as Mercedes' Magic Body Control system. No car. No road. Just chickens, accompanied to "Heads Will Roll" by Yeah Yeah Yeahs.

## Why it worked
The chicken's natural head-stabilization reflex is a perfect, universally recognizable demonstration of the technology principle. The absurdist choice — chickens in a car ad — stops the scroll instantly. The joke lands in three seconds; the product truth is embedded in the joke. Pure P14 (product as proof) delivered through P11 (absurd carrier).

## Steal
Find the clearest natural analogy for a technical product benefit, even if the analogy seems wildly unrelated. A perfect analogy is more credible than a perfect demo. Make the analogy the ad, not the support for the ad.
