# Billie Jean King Your Shoes (Adidas · 2019)

## Insight

Gender pay gaps in sports are well-documented — but they rarely feel personal or immediate. What if the gap was made visible in the most ordinary workplace interaction?

## Mechanic

Campaign inviting people to confront workplace gender pay gaps by "Billie Jean King-ing" their shoes — a mechanic where women could send their work shoes to Adidas and have them replaced with equal-value shoes representing what they'd earn if paid the same as male colleagues.

## Why it worked

Absurdist participation meets user-as-co-author: by making the shoe a physical proxy for pay equity, the campaign turned an abstract economic issue into a tactile, personal action. Billie Jean King as symbol was earned, not borrowed.

## Steal

Find the physical object in your category that can stand in for a social inequality. Make the object the proof. Exchange mechanics work because they require real commitment from the participant.
