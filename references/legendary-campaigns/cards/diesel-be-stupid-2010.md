# Be Stupid (Diesel · 2010)

## Insight
Smart people have good reasons not to take risks. Stupid people do things anyway. Every great thing — every real experience, every moment of genuine aliveness — requires a moment of apparent stupidity. Intelligence is the enemy of courage.

## Mechanic
A global campaign across film, OOH, print, and digital reframing stupidity as a philosophy of life. "Smart had one good idea and that idea was stupid." Images of beautiful people doing reckless, joyful, alive things. Manifestos rather than ads. The brand positioned as the choice of people who act before they think.

## Why it worked
The inversion of "smart = good" was genuinely counterintuitive for a luxury fashion brand — the category typically signals intelligence and taste. Diesel claimed the opposite: stupidity as the prerequisite for authentic experience. The target audience (young, risk-taking, experience-hungry) heard it as permission, not provocation.

## Steal
Inverting a cultural virtue (intelligence, responsibility, safety) works when the target audience secretly believes the inversion is true — they just haven't heard anyone say it out loud. "Be Stupid" articulated what Diesel's customer already felt: that overthinking kills living.
