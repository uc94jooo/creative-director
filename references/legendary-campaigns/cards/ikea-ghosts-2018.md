# Ghosts (IKEA · 2018)

## Insight
IKEA furniture is so ubiquitous it inhabits spaces its owners barely notice. The absurdist premise: even ghosts, given the chance to haunt somewhere, would choose IKEA furniture for its familiarity and comfort.

## Mechanic
A short film treating IKEA's home environment as the default setting even for supernatural beings — ghosts choosing IKEA products for comfort. The domesticity of IKEA and the otherworldliness of ghosts create the comedy.

## Why it worked
P11 (absurd as carrier) with a soft supernatural premise. IKEA's established visual world is strong enough to absorb the incongruity of ghosts without losing brand coherence. The premise is a gentle joke about how thoroughly IKEA has colonised the concept of "home."

## Steal
When your brand's products are so associated with a concept (home, comfort, domesticity) that even impossible scenarios default to them, that cultural saturation is the premise for absurdist comedy.
