# A Little Less Gravity / Defy (Nike · 2012)

## Insight
The sensation of peak athletic performance — the moment when effort disappears and movement feels effortless — is physically impossible to convey in words or through a product claim. It must be felt, not explained.

## Mechanic
A film using visual effects to create the impression of reduced gravity — athletes performing their movements with an otherworldly lightness that suggests the sensation of peak performance from inside the athlete's experience.

## Why it worked
The gravity effect externalizes an internal experience: what peak form feels like to the person inside it. This is categorically different from showing what performance looks like from the outside. The visual metaphor is simple, universally legible, and physically impossible to experience without doing what the athlete does.

## Steal
When the product delivers an internal experience (confidence, focus, physical sensation), find a visual metaphor that externalizes it. The metaphor must be immediately readable, not explained.
