# High School Girl (Shiseido · 2016)

## Insight

Makeup is powerful enough to transform anyone — in Japan, where beauty standards and gender conformity are particularly rigid, that transformative power is more radical than the industry typically acknowledges.

## Mechanic

Film following what appears to be five attractive high school girls — revealed at the end to be five middle-aged adult men who had been made up by Shiseido. The twist demonstrated makeup's transformative power with absolute precision.

## Why it worked

Absurdism meets design as idea: the reveal was not just a trick — it was the product demonstration. The reaction of audiences confronting their assumptions about gender and beauty did Shiseido's brief better than any claim. The film spread globally because the reveal demanded to be shared.

## Steal

If your product transforms something, find the most extreme possible demonstration of that transformation. The audience's gasp of recognition is the brief being completed. The more their assumptions are implicated, the more they share.
