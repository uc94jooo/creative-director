# Post-9/11 Broadway Tribute (MetLife · 2001)

## Insight
New York's Broadway district was the heart of the city's identity before September 11. A tribute anchored there carried weight that no generic memorial could — it was specific, local, and earned.

## Mechanic
A film tribute using Broadway's visual and cultural heritage — the marquees, the street, the performers — as the location for commemoration. Minimal copy, maximum specificity to place. The geography did the emotional work.

## Why it worked
Grief needs specificity to feel real. A generic memorial film about "America" would have been invisible. Anchoring the tribute to a specific street, a specific district, a specific cultural identity gave it emotional density.

## Steal
In memorial communication, specificity is respect. Generic remembrance is forgettable. Name the street. Name the people. The narrower the geography, the wider the feeling.
