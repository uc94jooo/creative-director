# The Rose That Grew From Concrete (Powerade · 2014)

## Insight
Sports drinks advertise to champions, but the actual lived experience of athletic aspiration is overcoming circumstances that shouldn't produce champions. The athletes who fight hardest are rarely the ones shown in the advertising.

## Mechanic
A long-form film drawing on Tupac Shakur's poem "The Rose That Grew From Concrete" as a framework for stories of athletes who defied socioeconomic or physical adversity to compete at high levels. The Powerade brand is positioned as a supporter of overlooked determination.

## Why it worked
The Tupac poem provides cultural legitimacy and emotional depth outside the conventional sports advertising grammar. By centering adversity rather than achievement, Powerade found a human truth that Nike and Gatorade's performance-first messaging left unclaimed.

## Steal
In a category where everyone shows the finish line, own the race. Find the human moment before achievement — the specific adversity, the specific doubt — and make that the campaign. Achievement without context is just a highlight reel.
