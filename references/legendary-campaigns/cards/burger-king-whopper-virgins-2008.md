# Whopper Virgins (Burger King · 2008)

## Insight
Every burger comparison ad was made with American consumers who had opinions, brand preferences, and cultural conditioning. What if you gave the Whopper to people who had never heard of McDonald's or Burger King — no brand bias, just taste?

## Mechanic
CP+B sent documentary teams to remote villages in Romania, Thailand, and Greenland — communities where fast food had never reached. Local "Whopper virgins" tried both a Whopper and a Big Mac for the first time. Their reactions were filmed. The Whopper won in unscripted taste tests. A 30-minute documentary and TV spots aired the results.

## Why it worked
The challenge with taste-test advertising is that viewers distrust it — they assume it's staged with brand loyalists or screened results. Going to people who had never heard of either brand removed that objection structurally. The absurdism (flying a film crew to Greenland to feed someone a Whopper) also made the campaign audacious and PR-worthy. The idea was simultaneously product proof (P14) and entertainment through the preposterousness of the premise (P11).

## Steal
When category comparison claims are universally distrusted, remove the variable everyone suspects: brand bias. The more extreme the measure to eliminate it, the more credible the claim. The stunt quality also earns free media that the production budget could never buy directly.
