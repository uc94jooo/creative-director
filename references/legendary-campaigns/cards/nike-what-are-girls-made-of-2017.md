# What Are Girls Made Of? (Nike Russia · 2017)

## Insight
In Russia, a traditional nursery rhyme assigns girls sugar and spice — passive, sweet qualities. Female athletes in Russia negotiate this cultural framing every day. Nike's Russian campaign had a specific cultural text to invert.

## Mechanic
A film rewriting the traditional Russian "What Are Girls Made Of?" nursery rhyme — replacing the sugar-and-spice answer with the actual stuff of female athletes: sweat, determination, blood, sacrifice, muscle. Specifically Russian cultural reference, universally legible insight.

## Why it worked
P13 (truth-telling) with a specific cultural target: not "girls can be athletes" in the generic global sense, but "the Russian story about girls is wrong." The nursery rhyme gives the campaign a specific text to argue with — which is always more powerful than arguing with a general attitude. The inversion is precise: same structure, opposite content.

## Steal
Find the specific cultural text — a nursery rhyme, a proverb, a saying — that encodes the attitude you're fighting. Invert the text exactly rather than making a general counter-argument. The precision of the target makes the response more powerful.
