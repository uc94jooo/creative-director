# Breaking2 (Nike · 2017)

## Insight
Human limits are not facts — they are narratives. The two-hour marathon had existed for decades as a story about what humans cannot do. Nike understood that attempting the impossible, not achieving it, is the more culturally resonant act.

## Mechanic
Nike recruited three elite runners — Eliud Kipchoge, Lelisa Desisa, Zersenay Tadese — to attempt a sub-two-hour marathon on a Formula 1 circuit in Monza. The attempt was live-streamed globally on Twitter. Kipchoge ran 2:00:25 — missing the record, but making it the most watched athletic attempt in history. The full documentary aired on National Geographic.

## Why it worked
P14 (product as proof) at civilisational scale: Nike's running technology is not a claim but a physical experiment with the world watching. The near-miss was narratively better than success — it kept the attempt human and honest. P16 (long-form drama) gave it a story arc that a product demo never has: tension, sacrifice, physics, failure at the edge of success. Nike did not sponsor the story; Nike was the story.

## Steal
Commission an attempt, not an ad. The process of trying, documented rigorously, is more credible than any claim. Build the story around the edge case where your product must perform at its absolute limit — the limit is where trust is earned.
