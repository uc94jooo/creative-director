# Breaking News (New Zealand Police · 2018)

## Insight
Following the viral success of the recruitment Bollywood video, the NZ Police had established a brand identity built on self-aware absurdism. Maintaining that tone in new executions is both easier (permission established) and harder (can't repeat the same joke).

## Mechanic
A follow-up recruitment film in the same absurdist tradition, presenting police work through a "breaking news" format that subverts the seriousness of news broadcasting. Part of the ongoing NZ Police content strategy established by "Freeze."

## Why it worked
The tonal consistency across executions builds a brand character rather than a one-off stunt. The audience knows what to expect from NZ Police content — and that expectation, combined with the surprise of each new execution, is the ongoing engagement mechanic.

## Steal
When a tonal identity has been established and validated by audience response, build on it rather than proving it again. The second execution should surprise within a known frame, not re-establish the frame.
