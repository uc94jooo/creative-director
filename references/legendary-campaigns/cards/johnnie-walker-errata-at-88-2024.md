# Errata at 88 (Johnnie Walker · 2024)

## Insight
In 1962, Bossa Nova was introduced to the world at Carnegie Hall by its founding fathers — all white men. Its founding mother, a pioneering Black Brazilian singer, wasn't invited. Sixty years later, history was being celebrated again, and she was left out a second time. The insight: a brand that celebrates personal journeys is obligated to correct the journeys that were stolen.

## Mechanic
Johnnie Walker Brazil used the journalistic format of an "errata" — a published correction of errors — to publicly acknowledge a historical injustice and correct it in real time. They bought two full pages in Brazil's largest newspapers formatted as formal corrections of the historical record, celebrating the singer's entire career. They then paid for her participation in the commemorative Carnegie Hall concert, where she became the only artist to receive a standing ovation exceeding three minutes. The errata campaign ran on social media, was shared by millions including major celebrities, and transformed the 88-year-old singer's life overnight.

## Why it worked
P17 (Cause as Core): the brand's stated purpose of celebrating personal journeys gave them legitimate standing to act, not just speak — they funded real participation, not symbolic recognition. P06 (Earned Media Trap): the combination of newspaper errata format, the Carnegie Hall standing ovation, and the emotional weight of a neglected 88-year-old legend receiving overdue recognition created unstoppable earned media. The campaign avoided the trap of performative purpose by doing something irreversible and costly that the brand didn't have to do.

## Steal
When correcting a historical injustice, use a structural format from the world that created the injustice — the newspaper correction, the official acknowledgment — to make the correction feel institutional rather than branded. And spend actual money on the actual fix, not on the ad about the fix.
