# Dumb Ways to Die (Metro Trains Melbourne · 2012)

## Insight
Rail safety campaigns use fear to change behaviour — and fail, because people habituate to fear-based messaging and disengage. The insight: the same information delivered through joy and absurdism reaches an audience that has learned to tune out warnings.

## Mechanic
An animated music video of cute cartoon characters dying in increasingly absurd ways, culminating in three deaths caused by train-safety stupidity. The song became a global hit, the video was shared hundreds of millions of times, and the characters became a franchise — apps, merchandise, sequels. A rail safety campaign that people chose to consume.

## Why it worked
P11 (absurd as carrier) at maximum strength: death — the most serious subject — delivered as cheerful animation with a catchy tune. The cognitive dissonance between cute visual execution and the actual subject (gruesome death) creates the laugh, the share, and the memory. P10 (serialization): the characters became a recurring franchise that kept the safety message alive long after the initial launch. The campaign reduced platform incidents by 30% in the first year.

## Steal
When your audience has become immune to the conventional register of your message (fear, urgency, warning), switch to the least appropriate register. The more serious the underlying message, the greater the attention earned by treating it as comedy.
