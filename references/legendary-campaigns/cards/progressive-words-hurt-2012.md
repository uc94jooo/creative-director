# Words Hurt (Progressive · 2012)

## Insight
Insurance industry jargon — "deductible," "premium," "co-pay," "exclusion" — causes genuine pain to people who don't understand it and can't afford not to. Making that pain literal and visible turns a dry frustration into shareable comedy.

## Mechanic
A film featuring characters being physically struck or wounded by insurance terminology — visual literalism applied to the painful language of the category. The comedic register frames Progressive as the brand that makes insurance understandable.

## Why it worked
Turning abstract consumer frustration (confusing jargon) into physical comedy creates shareability while communicating a product benefit (clarity). The absurdism was grounded in a real and universal consumer pain.

## Steal
Consumer frustrations that are real but invisible (jargon, complexity, hidden costs) become shareable when literalised. Find the abstract pain and make it physical — the externalisation is both the joke and the insight.
