# Sorry, I Spent It on Myself (Harvey Nichols · 2012)

## Insight
Buying luxury for yourself while giving others cheap gifts for Christmas is something Harvey Nichols shoppers actually do but would never admit publicly.

## Mechanic
A film and product line: Harvey Nichols created actual terrible gift products (a bag of gravel, a roll of sticky tape, a paper clip) sold as gifts, so shoppers could spend their real budget on themselves. Launched with a film depicting the social performance of gift-giving.

## Why it worked
The campaign validated a real behaviour that luxury shoppers already practiced but felt guilty about, turning the guilt into a joke and the product into the punchline. The actual product range made it commercially executable, not just provocative advertising.

## Steal
Identify the guilty, unspoken behaviour your best customers already practice. Name it, celebrate it, and create a product or service that enables it with brand endorsement. The audience rewards honesty about what they actually do.
