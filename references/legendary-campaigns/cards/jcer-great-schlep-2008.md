# The Great Schlep (JCER · 2008)

## Insight
Jewish grandparents in Florida were a decisive voting bloc for Obama — but they were being influenced more by what their grandchildren said than by any political advertising. The shortest path to winning Florida was through family dinner tables, not TV.

## Mechanic
Sarah Silverman starred in a video instructing young Jewish Americans to visit their grandparents in Florida and convince them to vote for Obama. Absurdist, irreverent, and culturally specific — it used guilt, love, and comedy to assign a political mission. The call to action was a plane ticket and a weekend.

## Why it worked
It found the real influence map, not the assumed one. Political communication usually goes direct to voters; this went to the people voters actually listen to. Silverman's tone — funny but sincere — gave cover for an earnest message that would have been insufferable if played straight.

## Steal
Before building a broadcast campaign, map the actual social influence network. Who do your targets actually change their behaviour for? Often it's not your media plan.
