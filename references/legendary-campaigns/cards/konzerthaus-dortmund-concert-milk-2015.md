# Concert Milk (Konzerthaus Dortmund · 2015)

## Insight
Classical music is perceived as élite and intimidating to those who haven't grown up with it. The Konzerthaus wanted to reach people in their daily routines — before they'd made the decision to not go.

## Mechanic
Milk cartons distributed through supermarkets functioned as miniature concert programs and recruitment tools — turning the everyday breakfast ritual into a point of contact with classical music. The carton design brought concert scheduling and artist information to a non-concert context.

## Why it worked
The design object traveled into a domestic, low-barrier space where classical music would never normally appear. The carton became a cultural ambassador, reaching people who would never pick up a brochure. The design did the work the advertising couldn't.

## Steal
When the audience won't come to you, design something that travels to them through an existing daily ritual. Find the mundane object that shares a context with the behavior you're trying to change.
