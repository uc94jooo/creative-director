# This is Wholesome (Honey Maid · 2014)

## Insight
"Wholesome" had been co-opted by a narrow, nostalgic image of the American family that excluded most actual families. Reclaiming that word for real families — interracial, same-sex, single-parent — was both commercially smart and culturally necessary.

## Mechanic
A TV film featuring non-traditional American families — a same-sex couple with children, an interracial family, a tattooed father — with the tagline "This is Wholesome." When the ad drew backlash, Droga5 printed every negative comment and used them to build a sculpture spelling "Love." The response to the backlash became a second campaign more viral than the first.

## Why it worked
The original campaign was brave. The response to backlash was masterful. By making the negative comments visible and physically transforming them into their opposite, Honey Maid demonstrated its position under fire — which is when brand values actually mean something.

## Steal
A brand's response to backlash can be more defining than its original campaign. Design the counter-move before launch: if the work attracts controversy, what does an on-brand response look like that proves the point rather than retreats from it?
