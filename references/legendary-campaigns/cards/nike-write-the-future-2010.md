# Write the Future (Nike · 2010)

## Mechanic
A three-minute Super Bowl film for the 2010 World Cup showing the branching consequences of a single moment in football — what happens to a player, a country, a civilization if one shot goes in or misses. Featuring Wayne Rooney, Didier Drogba, Cristiano Ronaldo, Fabio Cannavaro. Each decision branches into an alternate history of glory or disgrace.

## Insight
The World Cup is not just a sporting event — it is a moment that rewrites nations' stories of themselves. One kick can determine what a country believes about itself for years. Nike's athletes are not playing a game; they are writing history.

## Why it worked
The branching timeline structure makes the stakes of football explicitly civilizational, not sporting. The film earns its three-minute length through escalation — each branch going further into absurdist consequence. The craft (cameos, period detail, parallel cutting) demonstrates Nike's investment in the sport at the level of the sport itself.

## Steal
For global sporting events with genuine cultural stakes, don't show the game — show what the game decides about the world. Escalating consequence beyond the field of play is the only way to match the actual emotional weight of the event for committed fans.
