# The Best Job in the World (Tourism Queensland · 2009)

## Insight
Queensland's tourism board had a small budget but needed global coverage. The insight: if they created a genuine job opportunity — one that was objectively the best job in the world — the world's media would cover it for free. The product (the islands of Queensland) would become the prize rather than the subject of advertising.

## Mechanic
A real job listing for "Island Caretaker" of Hamilton Island, Great Barrier Reef: six months, AU$150,000, a luxury villa, and the responsibility of exploring the islands and blogging about it. The job was advertised through a single newspaper ad in 11 countries. The global response was 34,000 video applications from 200 countries. Every major news outlet covered it. The successful candidate generated 6 months of authentic content. Total advertising spend: AU$1.2 million. Earned media value: AU$400+ million.

## Why it worked
P09 (user as co-author) at scale: 34,000 applicants created campaign content, each sharing their video application. P04 (brand as activist) implicit: Tourism Queensland made a real commitment (a real salary, a real job, a real villa) which gave the stunt credibility. The mechanic was perfect — a genuine opportunity that could only exist in Queensland, demonstrating the destination through the act of creating the job rather than through advertising the destination. Every journalist who wrote "would you apply for this job?" was writing a Queensland tourism ad.

## Steal
When your product is genuinely desirable, the most powerful campaign is not advertising the product but creating an opportunity that proves it. Make the product the prize and let the audience compete for it. The competition becomes the campaign.
