# Stolen Idea (FedEx · 2006)

## Insight
Business culture runs on ideas — but ideas without execution are worthless. FedEx's comedy spots operated in the territory of business absurdism: what happens when things go wrong because someone didn't use FedEx.

## Mechanic
An absurdist comedy spot (P11) showing the consequences of an idea being "stolen" — likely within a corporate context — as a comedic demonstration of why reliable delivery matters. The enemy mechanic (P02) operates implicitly: unreliable logistics is the villain.

## Why it worked
FedEx's long-running campaign of office comedy spots (including the famous "caveman" and "Monday" ads) established a register of knowing, wry business humor. "Stolen Idea" extends this territory. The absurdism is grounded in recognizable business anxiety, making it specific enough to resonate with the B2B audience.

## Steal
B2B humor works when it targets the specific anxieties of the business audience — not generic workplace comedy but the precise fears (credit-stealing, unreliable systems, missed deadlines) that business people experience daily.
