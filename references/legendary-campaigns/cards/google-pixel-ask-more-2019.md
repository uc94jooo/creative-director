# Ask More (Google Pixel · 2019)

## Insight
People ask their phones for facts but rarely for genuine help with the moments that matter most. The Google Assistant — and by extension the Pixel — could be positioned as a partner in life's real questions, not just its trivial ones.

## Mechanic
Film showing people asking Google Assistant genuinely difficult, personal, and emotionally loaded questions — not just weather queries but questions about parenthood, loss, fear, connection. The phone as companion in real life.

## Why it worked
Long-form drama (P16): emotional resonance requires time — each question and its implications take time to land. The positioning of a technology product around human vulnerability rather than technical capability is rare enough to create differentiation. "Ask more" as invitation rather than instruction.

## Steal
Technology brands that position around human need rather than technical specification find more emotional territory. The question is more interesting than the answer — build campaigns around what your audience is genuinely afraid to ask.
