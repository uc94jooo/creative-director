# Nike+ FuelBand (Nike · 2012)

## Insight
Motivation to exercise is not a content problem — it is a feedback and measurement problem. The athlete inside every human needs a number to beat, a streak to maintain, a community to belong to. Technology could manufacture that accountability structure for anyone.

## Mechanic
A wristband that tracked physical activity using a proprietary metric ("NikeFuel") — universal, not category-specific, applicable to any movement. Combined with a social platform that turned personal activity into shared data: streaks, challenges, community rankings. The product created daily habits and generated organic sharing content through personal data.

## Why it worked
The wristband was not advertising — it was a product that solved the actual behaviour change problem. By inventing NikeFuel (a universal activity currency), Nike made all physical activity comparable across sports, age, and ability level. The community generated the motivation; the product generated the community; the brand owned the category.

## Steal
When the brand's purpose is behaviour change, the most effective medium is a product that makes the desired behaviour measurable and social. Advertising describes the change; a product produces it. Design the product first.
