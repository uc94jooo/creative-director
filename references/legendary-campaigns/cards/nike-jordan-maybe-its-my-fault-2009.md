# Maybe It's My Fault (Nike Jordan Brand · 2009)

## Insight
Michael Jordan created an impossible standard of excellence that has defined NBA expectations for two decades. Younger players are constantly measured against him. That comparison is both tribute and burden — and Jordan himself has never publicly addressed it.

## Mechanic
A film in which Michael Jordan speaks directly to camera, breaking his characteristic silence, and takes responsibility for the impossibly high standard he set: "Maybe I should have been more careful about what I made you believe." A confession of accidental damage alongside a manifesto for the next generation's own greatness.

## Why it worked
Jordan's silence was legendary — he almost never gave this kind of direct address. The film is structured as an apology, which is the opposite of what a brand ambassador typically delivers. The unexpected emotional register (regret, responsibility) makes it far more powerful than any celebration of achievement. The twist is that taking responsibility for the standard also affirms that the standard was real.

## Steal
When your greatest asset has become an intimidating burden to your audience, have them acknowledge it directly. The confession of power is itself an act of power. Vulnerability from someone who is never expected to be vulnerable is disproportionately credible.
