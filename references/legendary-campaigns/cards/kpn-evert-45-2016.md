# Evert_45 (KPN Telecom · 2016)

## Insight

Technology connects people across distance and time — but for elderly people who lived through WWII, this means something specific: they can connect with the next generation who has never experienced war, and those young people can learn from someone who has.

## Mechanic

KPN created a Twitter account for 91-year-old WWII veteran Evert Willemsz — staffed by KPN employees who helped him tweet daily in the 70 days leading up to Liberation Day. Young Dutch people discovered, followed, and replied. A bridge across generations built through a telecom platform.

## Why it worked

Tech as canvas meets user as co-author: the technology wasn't the hero — the human story was. But the technology (Twitter's real-time, public, participatory format) made the intergenerational conversation possible at scale. KPN demonstrated its platform's meaning rather than its features.

## Steal

Find someone whose story needs to be told, who cannot tell it through modern channels without help. Build the infrastructure for that story. Your brand is the bridge, not the storyteller.
