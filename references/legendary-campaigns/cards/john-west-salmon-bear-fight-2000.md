# Bear Fight (John West Salmon · 2000)

## Insight
"It's the fish John West reject that makes John West the best" is a product quality claim so familiar it's meaningless. Making the lengths John West goes to — literally fighting a bear for the best salmon — visible and absurd makes the claim unforgettable.

## Mechanic
A film opening with a plausible nature documentary of a bear catching salmon — then cutting to a fisherman who fights the bear hand-to-hand (including low groin kicks and hair pulling) to get the best fish. The logo: "John West. It's the salmon John West rejects that makes John West the best."

## Why it worked
The nature documentary opening was a deliberate tonal misdirection — the familiar register made the absurd break land harder. The product claim was unchanged; only the dramatisation was new. The fight choreography was committed enough to be genuinely funny and technically impressive.

## Steal
The oldest product claims can be refreshed not by changing the claim but by radicalising the dramatisation. If the claim is "we only take the best," show the most extreme version of that commitment. The audience will remember the bear fight long after they've forgotten the tagline.
