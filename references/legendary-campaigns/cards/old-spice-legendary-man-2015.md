# Legendary Man (Old Spice · 2015)

## Insight
Old Spice's absurdist masculine mythology — established by "The Man Your Man Could Smell Like" — was a brand platform flexible enough to generate new characters and escalations indefinitely. Each new execution extends the mythology rather than restating the original.

## Mechanic
A new iteration in Old Spice's absurdist masculine mythology — introducing or extending a character archetype of hyperbolically legendary masculinity, delivered in the brand's signature deadpan-absurdist register with production values that support the comedic escalation.

## Why it worked
Absurd as carrier (P11): the established brand tone allows each execution to pick up where the last left off without re-establishing the joke. Long-form drama (P16): the character study format rewards investment — the longer the film runs, the deeper the absurdism goes. Brand platform thinking at its most functional: the same creative logic generates infinite executions.

## Steal
Build a tonal platform broad enough to generate new characters rather than new concepts. A sufficiently defined tone of voice means the concept writes itself — you only need new characters, contexts, or escalations.
