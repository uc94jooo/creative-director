# Duct Tape (Miller Genuine Draft · 2000)

## Insight
The pragmatic ingenuity of everyday American life — fixing things with whatever's at hand — is a cultural identity marker that beer brands almost never touch. Practicality and beer drinking coexist in the same person; the category only shows one side.

## Mechanic
An Errol Morris documentary portrait featuring a person with strong convictions about duct tape as a universal solution, delivered with the same earnestness as testimony. The Miller beer appears as ambient context in the world of the character.

## Why it worked
The character's devotion to a mundane object (duct tape) makes them eccentric and lovable simultaneously. The beer brand benefits from the association with this unpretentious, resourceful worldview — positioning MGD as the beer of people who are honest about who they are.

## Steal
Build brand character through devotion to irrelevant specifics. A character who cares enormously about something small reveals more about values than any category-standard aspiration. The specific beats the universal.
