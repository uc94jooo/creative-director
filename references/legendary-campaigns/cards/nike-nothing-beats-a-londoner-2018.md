# Nothing Beats a Londoner (Nike · 2018)

## Insight
London youth are underrepresented in aspirational Nike communication — which is typically American, globally polished, and celebrity-led. But London sport is specific, varied, and deeply local — kids training in car parks, swimming pools, rooftops. The campaign needed to celebrate that specificity without making it feel quaint.

## Mechanic
A three-minute film featuring 258 London young athletes — from the obscure to the famous — each one-upping the last with increasingly specific London sports situations. Cameos from established athletes (Harry Kane, Mo Farah, Raheem Sterling) appear not as heroes but as participants in the same London specificity as the unknowns. A parallel social challenge invited London athletes to respond with their own stories.

## Why it worked
P16 (long-form) at the service of P09 (user as co-author): the film was the engine that made the social challenge credible. The casting — 258 real people — was the creative act. No other city could run this film; its irreproducibility is its strength. Cannes Grand Prix validated that hyperlocal specificity beats global aspiration in both authenticity and earned media.

## Steal
When global brand communication ignores a specific city or culture, that culture's specificity is yours to own. The more specific the casting, the more universal the recognition — because every city has its version of this truth.
