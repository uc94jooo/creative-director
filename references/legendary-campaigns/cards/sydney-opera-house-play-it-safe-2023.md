# Play It Safe (Sydney Opera House · 2023)

## Insight
The Opera House is perceived as a venue for safe, established art — a place people feel they should go but don't because it feels intimidating or irrelevant. But the institution's actual programming is anything but safe.

## Mechanic
A full musical film written and performed by Tim Minchin: a sardonic song celebrating the choice to "play it safe" — get a safe job, date safe people, never take artistic risks — with the implicit argument that the Opera House is for people who refuse. Cinematic quality, no product exposition, irony as the primary register.

## Why it worked
An institution using self-satire to challenge its own perceived stuffiness is structurally unusual and almost impossible to ignore. Long-form drama (P16) gave the musical enough runtime to build genuine comedic momentum. Truth-telling (P13): naming the audience's actual anxiety about the venue directly rather than papering over it with aspirational imagery.

## Steal
Heritage institutions can use self-deprecating irony to dismantle the intimidation barrier — but only if the irony is genuine and the content is actually as good as the institution claims. The film must be better than what people expected.
