# Dear Sophie (Google · 2011)

## Insight
Google's products are used in every important human moment — but the brand had never made that claim through an actual human story. The insight: a father documents his daughter's entire childhood through Gmail drafts, photos, YouTube videos, and Google searches — and never once does Google feel like the subject.

## Mechanic
A two-minute film shows a father creating a Gmail account for his newborn daughter "Sophie" and populating it with milestones: first words, first steps, first day of school — each documented using different Google products. The campaign's P14 mechanic is the Google suite itself: every product shown is used as it actually exists, demonstrating utility through genuine emotional purpose. P16's long-form format allows the emotional accumulation to build across a childhood compressed into two minutes.

## Why it worked
Product demonstration (P14) achieved at human scale: instead of features, you see use cases that matter. The film is not about Google — it's about a father's love expressed through Google. The emotional truth carries the brand, not the other way around. "Dear Sophie" became one of the most-cited examples of product demonstration done through storytelling rather than specification.

## Steal
Show your product being used in the moments that matter most to people's lives. The product becomes the medium for human connection — and the brand inherits the emotional weight of that connection.
