# Terry Tate: Office Linebacker (Reebok · 2003)

## Insight
Office life is full of petty violations — leaving food in the break room, not replacing the paper in the printer, scheduling unnecessary meetings. Everyone fantasizes about consequences. The insight: what if there were a linebacker who physically tackled office violators with NFL-level force?

## Mechanic
A series of short films featuring Terry Tate, a professional linebacker who works in an office. When colleagues commit minor workplace infractions, Tate tackles them with full force through office furniture, trash cans, and colleagues, shouting extended rants about their violations. Shot in a realistic office setting with real-looking documentary-style camera work. Aired in Super Bowl 2003 and became one of the first pre-YouTube viral videos.

## Why it worked
P11 (absurd as carrier): the physical incongruity — NFL aggression in an office setting — is the entire joke, and the joke never stops being funny because the violence is real-looking even as the premise is impossible. P16 (long-form drama): the films work as short character pieces — Tate has a personality, a relationship with the office, a code of conduct. Reebok became associated with athletic performance intensity applied to a comic civilian context.

## Steal
Place a character with extreme specialized competence in a context where that competence is wildly inappropriate. The comedy comes from the competence being deployed with full seriousness — Tate does not think his tacklings are funny.
