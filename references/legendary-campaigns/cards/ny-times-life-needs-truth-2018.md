# Life Needs Truth (The New York Times · 2018)

## Insight
Disinformation degrades more than political discourse — it degrades the shared substrate of reality that community, commerce, science, and daily life depend on. The Times frames truth not as a luxury but as a precondition for life as we know it.

## Mechanic
A campaign arguing that the capacity to agree on facts is foundational to all human endeavor — medicine, parenting, policy, love. Truth is framed not as an abstract virtue but as an operational necessity. Film format conveys the stakes through lived consequences.

## Why it worked
Truth telling (P13): the argument is made through concrete examples of what breaks down when truth is absent — more persuasive than abstract appeals to journalistic values. The framing as necessity rather than ideal makes the ask (subscribe, trust us) feel rational rather than righteous.

## Steal
When arguing for the importance of your category, argue from necessity not from virtue. "Life needs X" is a stronger frame than "X is good." What breaks down when your product disappears is more compelling than what it provides when present.
