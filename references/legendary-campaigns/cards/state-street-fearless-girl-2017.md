# Fearless Girl (State Street Global Advisors · 2017)

## Insight
Wall Street's Charging Bull — a symbol of financial aggression and masculine dominance — had stood unchallenged for 27 years. The absence of a counter to that symbol was itself a statement about corporate power. State Street needed to signal its commitment to gender diversity on corporate boards without a claim — only a physical act would be credible.

## Mechanic
A bronze statue of a small girl, hands on hips, staring down the Charging Bull — placed on Bowling Green, lower Manhattan, on International Women's Day 2017. No copy beyond a small plaque. The statue changed the meaning of an existing landmark without touching it. Social media did the rest: the Fearless Girl became one of the most photographed spots in New York City within 24 hours.

## Why it worked
P17 (design as idea): the entire campaign is one sculpture. No copy needed. P09 (user as co-author): millions of people photographed themselves with the Fearless Girl and shared — the campaign's media was the audience's participation. The genius is the contextual dependency: the girl means something only because of the bull. Remove the bull, remove the meaning. State Street did not add a statement to a space; they changed what an existing statement meant.

## Steal
Find a symbol in the culture that carries a meaning you want to challenge. Rather than attacking it, add something to its context that changes its meaning without changing the original. The addition says everything; the original does the work.
