# Activation Toolkit

Activations are not advertising. An ad campaign communicates something about a brand. An activation creates a moment, an object, a behavior, or a system that has value or meaning independent of the brand's message. The distinction matters because the creative logic is different: advertising starts from "what do we say?" Activation starts from "what do we do — and does it matter without us attached to it?"

This reference provides a framework for 9 activation formats, shared mechanic patterns, and routing logic for creative briefs that call for activation over advertising.

---

## The Critical Test: Non-advertising vs Execution

Before generating activation ideas, run one question:

> "Remove the campaign — does the activation still have meaning?"

- **Yes → `idea_type: non_advertising`**. The thing has standalone value (utility, cultural artifact, behavior, physical object). The brand is present but not the reason it exists.
- **No → `idea_type: execution`**. It is a creative vehicle for an ad message. Still valid, but use advertising logic — not this toolkit.

**Examples:**

| Activation | Test result | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Boards of Change (BLM protest signs → voter registration booths) | non_advertising | The booths worked without knowing who funded them |
| Guardian Beads (Vodafone SIM-embedded prayer beads, Kumbh Mela) | non_advertising | Functional safety object; brand is a component |
| Spotify Wrapped (annual personal listening report) | non_advertising | People share it because it reflects their identity, not Spotify |
| Oreo-patterned referee jerseys as scannable triggers | execution | Clever, but zero value without the promo mechanic |
| Standard "win a car" loyalty promo | execution | Pure incentive layer; remove brand, nothing remains |

Run this test in Phase 1 INTAKE when the brief mentions activation. Run it in Phase 4 when `idea_type` has been tagged.

## 9 Activation Formats

---

### 1. Experiential

**Mechanic:** Creates a physical or immersive moment people choose to enter — pop-up, installation, live environment. The experience is the product. Worth sharing is built in, not bolted on.

**Best for:** briefs requiring emotional brand reappraisal, launch with PR lift, cultural credibility building, high-consideration categories where trial matters.

**Pattern triggers:** P06 (Limitation as Power), P09 (User as Co-Author), P12 (Social Experiment), P14 (Product as Proof), P18 (Tech as Canvas)

**Canonical examples:**
- `experiential` — Adidas Liquid Billboard (Dubai, 2021): open-water swimming billboard for inclusive swimwear. The billboard was a functional pool. Media value = zero; PR value = global.
- `experiential` — VW Fun Theory / Piano Stairs: behavior change through pleasure, no signage needed.

**Risks:** scales poorly; build costs kill ROI on short-window activations; "instagrammable" brief produces hollow spectacle with no behavior change; weather, footfall dependency.

---

### 2. Shopper & Retail

**Mechanic:** Intervenes at the moment of decision — on-shelf, in-store, at checkout. Changes the frame of reference without additional media. Packaging, price display, shelf context, in-store environment are the canvas.

**Best for:** CPG briefs with no broadcast budget, shelf-switching objectives, trial of new SKU, category re-framing at point of purchase.

**Pattern triggers:** P01 (Format as Idea), P03 (Behavior Inversion), P11 (Absurd as Carrier), P17 (Design as Idea)

**Canonical examples:**
- `ooh_print` — Inglorious Fruits & Vegetables (Intermarché): imperfect produce given its own identity and 30% discount. Reduced food waste, increased traffic.
- `stunt_pr` — Rejected Ales: bad reviews printed on labels. Defect becomes the reason to buy.

**Risks:** retailer buy-in is a gatekeeping risk; works in-store but rarely travels beyond it; requires strong structural insight, not just clever copy.

---

### 3. Promo

**Mechanic:** Constructs a mechanic with a defined call-to-action, participation barrier, and reward. Value of the reward is secondary; the mechanic's creativity is the message.

**Best for:** trial generation, acquisition, social fuel, short-burst sales uplift; strongest when the mechanic itself earns coverage.

**Pattern triggers:** P03 (Behavior Inversion), P09 (User as Co-Author), P11 (Absurd as Carrier), P12 (Social Experiment), P14 (Product as Proof)

**Canonical examples:**
- `stunt_pr` — Whopper Sacrifice (BK, 2009): unfriend 10 Facebook friends to earn a free Whopper. 234K friends deleted before Facebook shut it down. Sacrifice + humor + social provocation.
- `digital_product` — Decode Jay-Z (Bing, 2010): pages of Jay-Z's autobiography hidden across the internet. Hunt format turns trial into culture.

**Risks:** high-effort mechanics kill participation rates; reward must feel worth it relative to barrier; legal/T&C complexity grows with scale; "win a prize" without a mechanic idea is not promo, it's noise.

---

### 4. PR Stunts

**Mechanic:** A single, discrete action designed to generate earned coverage — the stunt is the story. One clear act + an unexpected angle = an article journalists write themselves.

**Best for:** low-budget buzz, market entry, brand repositioning, rapid cultural response; any brief where earned media is the only realistic path to reach.

**Pattern triggers:** P02 (Enemy or Conflict), P04 (Brand as Activist), P05 (Cultural Hijack), P11 (Absurd as Carrier), P12 (Social Experiment)

**Canonical examples:**
- `stunt_pr` — Morning After Island (GEPAE Honduras): floating platform in neutral waters providing legal access to the morning-after pill. Location was the entire idea.
- `stunt_pr` — The American Takeover (ROM Romania): national chocolate brand "sold itself" to America. Public outrage. Brand reversed it. Became a national identity event.

**Risks:** one-day news cycle; culturally mistimed stunts become liabilities fast; without genuine stakes or originality, labeled a publicity stunt pejoratively; legal and reputational exposure on provocative territory.

---

### 5. Creative Commerce

**Mechanic:** The purchase is embedded in the experience — no selling moment is visible. Commerce appears as content, service, or utility. Conversion happens inside something people wanted anyway.

**Best for:** e-commerce and DTC briefs, app-driven brands, categories with high consideration where product demo is part of the sale.

**Pattern triggers:** P01 (Format as Idea), P09 (User as Co-Author), P14 (Product as Proof), P18 (Tech as Canvas)

**Canonical examples:**
- `digital_product` — Traffic Jam Whopper (BK Brazil, 2019): mobile order triggered when GPS detected driver in traffic jam. Product feature becomes activation.
- `digital_product` — Samsung iTest (BBH New Zealand, 2021): web app that turns an iPhone into a Samsung experience. Trial without a store visit.

**Risks:** tech dependency creates fragility; mechanic must be genuinely useful, not a gimmick; requires product that can carry the weight of the creative premise.

---

### 6. Social Media

**Mechanic:** Designed for organic spread — native to platform behavior, participatory by construction. The mechanic is structurally viral: it requires or invites others to do the same thing.

**Best for:** brand relevance with younger audiences, product launches needing cultural velocity, UGC acquisition, brand repositioning through cultural signals.

**Pattern triggers:** P05 (Cultural Hijack), P09 (User as Co-Author), P10 (Serialization & Ritual), P12 (Social Experiment)

**Viral mechanic formula (Ice Bucket pattern):**
1. Simple physical action
2. Visible outcome (filmable)
3. Pass-it-on structure (nominate next)
4. Reason beyond personal interest
5. Low entry barrier

**Canonical examples:**
- `social` — Ice Bucket Challenge (ALS, 2014): all five structural elements. 17M participants, $115M raised.
- `social` — Spotify Wrapped (annual): personal data as identity content. People share because it says something about them, not Spotify.

**Risks:** impossible to force virality; inauthenticity is immediately detected; trend-chasing without mechanic originality = content waste.

---

### 7. Events & Sponsorship

**Mechanic:** Activates within or around an owned event or third-party sponsorship — converts logo presence into behavior, memory, or earned content. The event is context; the activation is the value.

**Best for:** sports sponsors, festival brands, any brief where the brand has rights to an audience moment but no guaranteed attention.

**Pattern triggers:** P05 (Cultural Hijack), P06 (Limitation as Power), P09 (User as Co-Author), P10 (Serialization & Ritual)

**Canonical examples:**
- `experiential` — Mannequin Spectators (COVID-era): empty stadiums replaced with mannequins. Constraint became the concept.
- `stunt_pr` — Coors Lights Out (2024): Ohtani foul ball destroyed a Coors billboard. Within 48 hours, Coors released a can with the same black damage mark. Real-time, constraint as resource.

**Risks:** sponsorship rights don't guarantee relevance; most event activations are branded logistics, not ideas; official sponsors routinely out-activated by ambush competitors with better ideas.

---

### 8. Design & Packaging

**Mechanic:** The object — pack, label, material, structure — carries the idea without additional communication. Design functions as a medium, not decoration.

**Best for:** packaging redesign briefs, limited editions, social cause alignment where the product must do the work, categories where the pack is the primary brand touchpoint.

**Pattern triggers:** P01 (Format as Idea), P04 (Brand as Activist), P17 (Design as Idea)

**Canonical examples:**
- `ooh_print` — The Tampon Book (The Female Company, 2019): tampons packaged as a book to exploit a lower German VAT rate for books vs. hygiene products. Packaging as legal and political act.
- `ooh_print` — Diagnosticker (Lifebuoy Indonesia, 2022): soap packaging = thermometer + QR code to medical consultation. Pack as diagnostic tool.

**Risks:** requires structural insight, not visual novelty; limited to audiences who interact with the object; hard to scale beyond SKU.

---

### 9. B2B & Trade

**Mechanic:** Activates within a professional ecosystem — partners, distributors, trade buyers, industry. The audience is expert, skeptical of marketing, and responds to genuine utility, data, or credibility signals.

**Best for:** category leadership briefs, trade relationship building, market development programs, industry-first positioning.

**Pattern triggers:** P04 (Brand as Activist), P13 (Truth Telling), P14 (Product as Proof)

**Canonical examples:**
- `digital_product` — Recipe for Growth (iFood Brazil, 2025): operational data from billions of delivery orders packaged as a strategic tool for restaurant owners. Data already existed; framing it as a B2B product was the idea.
- `integrated` — Piñatex: material made from pineapple fiber, positioned as a pitch to fashion brands. The material is the activation.

**Risks:** long sales cycles; B2B activations rarely travel beyond the trade audience; content marketing often substitutes for genuine utility.

## Activation Mechanics Patterns

Recurring structural mechanics that appear across formats. Each can be applied to most of the 9 categories above.

| Pattern | Mechanic | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Sacrifice** | Ask the audience to give up something with social or personal value — proves commitment, creates conversation | Whopper Sacrifice: unfriend 10 people → get a burger |
| **Impossible Place** | Create or claim a location that should not exist | Morning After Island: legal medical access in international waters |
| **Data as Revelation** | Take existing data (brand or user), reframe it as identity or insight | Spotify Wrapped: your listening history = your personality |
| **Defect Reframing** | The flaw becomes the product's defining trait | Inglorious Fruits: ugly produce gets its own ad campaign |
| **Brand Utility** | Brand solves a real, present problem — not a brand problem | Guardian Beads: Vodafone SIM embedded in Kumbh Mela prayer beads for pilgrim safety |
| **Values Provocation** | Force a choice that exposes the audience's actual values | Whopper Sacrifice: friendship vs. food |
| **Limitation as Resource** | Physical or legal constraint becomes the creative premise | Minefields Honey: bees fly where humans cannot → post-war economy |
| **Product as Currency** | The product itself becomes the payment mechanism or access token | 0.0 Barriers: Heineken 0.0 can = toll road payment |
| **Hidden Prize** | Prize or value is concealed inside a commodity, only discoverable through participation | Rice of Glory: hand-painted prize grains hidden in packs |
| **Lottery Over Asset** | Declare an existing asset (ticket number, seat, timestamp) a lottery ticket — zero product change | Lucky Yatra: Indian Railways ticket number = existing lottery |
| **Hijack Own Asset** | Re-declare the meaning of something the brand already owns (logo, uniform, packaging) as a new mechanic | Handshake Hunt: Mercado Libre logo triggers a voucher on any TV handshake |
| **Promo as Policy** | The promotion is structured as a public position or legal intervention, not a sales tactic | SHT / Second-Hand Tax: IKEA Canada's 13% discount = petition to eliminate double HST |

## Decision Matrix

Match the brief signal to the most productive format(s):

| Brief signal | Primary format | Secondary format |
|---|---|---|
| "We need buzz, low budget" | pr_stunt | social |
| "Behaviour change objective" | experiential | brand_utility (via any format) |
| "We sponsor X, need activation" | events_sponsorship | stunt_pr |
| "Drive trial at retail" | shopper_retail | promo |
| "Virality / social-first" | social | promo |
| "B2B audience, credibility" | b2b_trade | design_packaging |
| "Commerce / conversion" | creative_commerce | digital_product |
| "Cultural relevance, brand repositioning" | stunt_pr | experiential |
| "Product is the idea" | design_packaging | creative_commerce |

| "Data asset we haven't used" | creative_commerce | social |

## Use in creative-director Skill

- **Phase 1 INTAKE:** load this reference when the brief mentions activation, stunt, utility, experiential, retail mechanic, gaming, sponsorship, or PR play. Use the Critical Test immediately to determine `idea_type` before generating any concepts.
- **Phase 4 EVALUATE + REFINE:** if `idea_type: non_advertising` has been tagged, apply the "Remove the campaign" test as a pass/fail gate before scoring. Ideas that fail the test should be reclassified as `execution` and evaluated under advertising logic, not activation logic.
